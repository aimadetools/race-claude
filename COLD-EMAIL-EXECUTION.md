# Cold Email Execution Guide for PricePulse Launch

**Estimated time:** 2-3 hours for batch 1 (25-50 founders)
**Expected ROI:** 2-5% open rate, 1-3 signups per 50 emails
**Priority:** High (Day 3+, after Show IH/Twitter momentum)

---

## Phase 1: Build Your Email List (30 min)

### Where to Find Founder Email Addresses

**Best sources (highest intent):**

1. **Twitter (fastest)**
   - Search SaaS founders in your space
   - Click on profiles, check if email is in bio or website
   - Example search: "founder SaaS pricing" + "Open to work"
   - Collect 10-15 profiles this way

2. **LinkedIn (most reliable)**
   - Search: "founder" + "SaaS" + industry filter (Productivity, Business Software, etc.)
   - Click on person → copy email from website link
   - Filter by people who recently changed jobs (higher likelihood to compare pricing)
   - Collect 20-30 contacts

3. **Hunter.io (automated lookup)**
   - Find company domain (example: linear.com, notion.so)
   - Use Hunter to find founder email (usually firstname@domain.com)
   - Free tier: 50/month. Paid: $50-500/month
   - Example: hunt "linear.com" → find founder email

4. **RocketReach (database)**
   - Search by company + job title
   - Free trial gives 50 searches
   - More reliable than manual lookup
   - Cost: $199/month (skip if budget tight)

5. **Founders in your existing network**
   - DM on Twitter: "Hey, can I add you to early feedback list for PricePulse?"
   - Much higher response rate (they know you)
   - Start with 10-15 warm intros

6. **IH/Twitter profiles in your niche**
   - Indie Hackers: Search "bootstrapped SaaS", copy founder names, find emails on personal sites
   - Twitter: Follow relevant hashtags (#IndieBiz #SaaS #Bootstrap), find founders

**Target profile for cold email:**
- Founded a B2B SaaS company (pricing is their problem)
- Raised <$10M (or bootstrapped) — they care about $19/mo tools
- Company is 2-5 years old (established enough to have competitor tracking need)
- Active on social media (Twitter/LinkedIn = warm up)

**How many to start?** 25-50 for batch 1. Quality > quantity. Better to have 20 great emails than 100 mediocre ones.

## Phase 2: Prepare Your List (20 min)

**Create a Google Sheet or CSV with these columns:**

| Email | First Name | Company | Competitor | Status | Response | Date |
|-------|-----------|---------|-----------|--------|----------|------|
| founder@company.com | Sam | Linear | Notion | Sent | Waiting | 4/23 |
| | | | | | | |

**Instructions:**
1. Paste all emails in column A
2. Extract first names (use formula or manual)
3. Add their company name
4. Add a competitor they likely care about (check their product)
5. Mark Status = "Unsent"
6. Track Response and Date as replies come in

**Why competitor column?** So you can personalize each email: "I know Linear and Notion compete a lot..."

## Phase 3: Personalize Templates (45 min)

**DO personalize:**
- [Company Name] → actual company name
- [Competitor Name] → their actual competitor
- [First Name] → use their actual first name
- [Your Name] → sign with your name
- Add 1-2 sentences about their company if possible

**DON'T over-personalize:**
- Don't write a 5-paragraph love letter
- Don't mention their latest product launch in detail
- Keep it short (3-4 paragraphs max)

**Example personalization:**

**Generic:**
```
Hi [First Name],

Do you track when [Competitor Name] changes their pricing?
...
```

**Personalized:**
```
Hi Sam,

Do you track when Notion changes their pricing?

I know Linear and Notion compete heavily in the workspace tools space. Notion moves fast on pricing — I've seen them restructure 3x in the last year.

Most founders don't notice until a customer mentions it in a call.
...
```

**How much better?** Personalized emails get 3-5x higher response rates than generic. Spend 1-2 min per email on personalization.

## Phase 4: Choose Your Sending Method

### Option A: Gmail (Free, Simplest)

**Pros:** No setup, free, built-in tracking (with Boomerang/HubSpot)
**Cons:** Limited per-day (50-100), slower, requires copying/pasting

**Setup:**
1. Keep Google Sheet open
2. Compose email in Gmail draft
3. Copy/paste personalized body
4. Send
5. Mark as "Sent" in spreadsheet
6. Move to next

**How to avoid spam folder:**
- Send 10-20 per day (not all at once)
- Space them 1-2 hours apart
- Include email signature
- Don't use ALL CAPS or spam trigger words

**Spam trigger words to avoid:**
- "URGENT"
- "FREE" (use "free tier" instead)
- "ACT NOW"
- "CLICK HERE"
- "$$" or multiple exclamation marks

### Option B: Mailchimp (Free for small lists)

**Pros:** Bulk sending, better deliverability, can schedule
**Cons:** Requires setup, limit 500/month free tier, looks less personal

**Setup:**
1. Go to mailchimp.com, create account
2. Create "Campaign" → "Regular email"
3. Add your list
4. Customize template
5. Send now or schedule

**Better for:** Batch 2+ when you have 100+ emails

### Option C: Substack/Ghost (if you have newsletter)

**Pros:** Looks professional, builds audience, free tier available
**Cons:** Overkill for cold outreach, people might unsubscribe immediately

**Skip this for cold email.** Use for warm announcements later.

**Recommendation:** Use **Gmail** for batch 1 (more personal, better response). Use **Mailchimp** for batch 2+ (speed).

## Phase 6: Track Responses (Daily, 10 min)

**Daily routine:**
1. Check email inbox for replies (filter for "PricePulse" or "getpricepulse")
2. Copy response to spreadsheet (Date + Response)
3. Reply immediately if warm response
4. Track in launch-metrics.html under "Cold Email Signups"

**Response types:**

| Type | Likelihood | Response |
|------|-----------|----------|
| Direct signup | 5-10% | "Thanks! Free tier available now →" |
| Question about product | 10-15% | Answer thoughtfully, send demo link |
| "Too expensive" | 5% | Send $19 plan offer, mention $0 free tier |
| No response | 70-80% | (Track for follow-up #1) |
| Unsubscribe/harsh | 1-2% | Don't reply, mark as "bad fit" |

**Example warm response:**
```
Subject: RE: Do you track when Notion changes pricing?

Hey Sam,

Thanks for asking! Yes, exactly — Notion changes pretty aggressively.

Free tier covers 2 competitors (daily checks). $19/mo for 10 competitors with hourly monitoring.

Check out the demo at https://getpricepulse.com/demo.html to see what the alerts look like.

Let me know if you have questions!

Best,
[Your Name]
```

## Troubleshooting & FAQ

**Q: Is cold email legal?**
A: Yes. You're sending promotional email. Include unsubscribe option in signature. Better: use Gmail (not mass mail service) to look personal.

**Q: Will Gmail flag me as spam?**
A: Unlikely if you:
- Send <50/day
- Space emails 30 min apart
- Don't use spam trigger words
- Include clear signature

**Q: Should I offer a discount?**
A: Not yet. Offer free tier first. Only discount if they ask price objection.

**Q: What if they say "too expensive"?**
A: Respond with "I built this specifically for bootstrapped founders because I was tired of $500/month tools. $19/mo is one coffee subscription. But I get it — free tier is always an option."

**Q: How do I measure success?**
A: Goal: 2-5% response rate, 1-3 signups per 50 emails. If lower, adjust subject line or add more competitor specificity.

**Q: When should I send batch 2?**
A: 3-4 days after batch 1 (so batch 1 is still getting replies). Repeat same process.

## Best Practices

1. **Sign with your real name**
   - People buy from people
   - "Best, [Your Name]" > "Sincerely, PricePulse Team"

2. **Include a real link**
   - Always include https://getpricepulse.com
   - Use same URL everywhere (build consistent brand)

3. **Be honest about stage**
   - "Just launched" = founder respects the hustle
   - "Early feedback welcome" = invites conversation
   - "Zero paying customers yet" = vulnerability builds trust

4. **Follow up, but don't be annoying**
   - First email: ask
   - Follow-up #1 (week 1): gentle reminder
   - Follow-up #2 (week 2): final attempt, ask different question
   - That's it — after 2 follow-ups, move on

5. **Track everything**
   - Which subject lines work best? (track open rates)
   - Which competitors does your ICP care about? (update templates)
   - Which time of day gets replies? (send batch 2 at same time)
