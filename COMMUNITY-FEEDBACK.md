# Community Feedback

Real feedback from humans who looked at PricePulse.

## Feedback #1 — Human operator review (May 3, 2026)

**Source:** Manual review of getpricepulse.com

**What works well:**
- The live tracker with 40 companies is genuinely useful. Real data, real changes. This is the strongest part of the product.
- Free tier (2 competitors, daily checks) is a smart acquisition funnel. Low friction.
- The SaaS Team Cost Calculator and Pricing Database are excellent free tools for SEO. 113+ companies in the database is solid content.
- The price hike leaderboard is a viral content play. GitHub Copilot +90% is a headline that gets shared.
- Clean diffs showing exactly what changed (not just that something changed) is a real differentiator vs Visualping.
- Pricing is reasonable and clearly positioned against Crayon (00+/mo).

**What needs work:**
- The comparison pages (vs Crayon, vs Visualping) need to actually work. Are these real comparisons or placeholder pages?
- "Slack coming soon", "Webhooks + API (soon)", "Zapier (soon)" — too many "coming soon" items. Either build them or remove the promises. Users see "coming soon" as "never".
- The 40 companies tracked — is this actually running? Can users verify the monitoring is real by checking the tracker page? If the tracker shows stale data, trust evaporates.
- No testimonials visible. You are further along than most race agents (real product, real data), but zero social proof. Even one tweet from a user saying "this caught a price change I missed" would be huge.
- The blog content needs distribution. 25+ articles sitting without traffic is wasted effort. Submit to Hacker News, post on relevant subreddits.

**Strategic observation:**
PricePulse is the most complete product in the race. It has real functionality, real data, and a clear value proposition. The gap is distribution and social proof. The price hike leaderboard is your best viral asset — lean into it harder. Consider a monthly "SaaS Price Hike Report" that you publish and share. That is the kind of content that gets picked up by newsletters and tech blogs.

**Action items:**
1. Verify the live tracker is actually updating. If it is stale, fix it immediately. This is your credibility.
2. Get one real user. Offer free Starter plans to 5 founders in exchange for feedback.
3. The monthly price hike report idea — this could be your distribution engine. Real data that people want to share.
4. Remove or deliver on "coming soon" features. Pick one (Slack) and ship it.
