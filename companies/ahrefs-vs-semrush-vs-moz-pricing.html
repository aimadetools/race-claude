Which Tool Should You Choose?

Choose Ahrefs If:

You need the most comprehensive backlink data and are analyzing high-volume sites. Ahrefs dominates backlink analysis with 192B+ indexed backlinks. Best for large agencies, ecommerce sites, and competitive keyword analysis. Trade-off: Highest starting price ($129/mo) with no annual discount.

Choose Semrush If:

You want an all-in-one marketing platform beyond SEO: PPC competitor intel, social media management, content marketing, and advertising tools. Semrush is the jack-of-all-trades. Best for agencies managing PPC + organic, content marketers, and teams needing integrated reporting. 20% annual discount helps offset the $139.95/mo starting price.

Choose Moz If:

You're budget-conscious and want a solid, balanced SEO tool without overpaying. Moz Standard ($99/mo, or $74.25/mo annual) is the cheapest entry and includes all core features: rank tracking, site audit, keyword research. Excellent for small agencies, solopreneurs, and SMBs. Biggest 25% annual discount of the three. Trade-off: Smaller backlink database (less useful for link analysis).

Frequently Asked Questions

Which has the best backlink data?

Ahrefs (192B+ backlinks), followed by Semrush (21B+), then Moz (1B+). If backlink analysis is your primary need, Ahrefs is worth the premium. For keyword research + rank tracking, Semrush and Moz are sufficient.

Can I use multiple tools?

Yes. Many agencies use Ahrefs for backlink analysis + Semrush for PPC/social. Or Ahrefs for research + Moz for team collaboration. Most tools export data, so switching is possible but time-intensive.

Which has the best rank tracking?

All three offer unlimited rank tracking at their base paid tiers. Ahrefs and Semrush have more granular data; Moz is simpler but sufficient for most teams.

What if I need PPC data?

Semrush is the only one with comprehensive PPC competitor intelligence. Ahrefs and Moz are SEO-only tools.

Which is best for small agencies?

Moz Standard ($99/mo or $74.25/mo annually) is the most budget-friendly. For full-service agencies needing PPC, Semrush wins. For backlink-heavy clients, Ahrefs is the answer.
