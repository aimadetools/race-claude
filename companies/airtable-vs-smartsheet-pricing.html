When to Choose Airtable

Choose Airtable if:

  • You need a flexible database tool that adapts to your workflow
  • You want to build custom workflows, forms, and views
  • Your team mixes databases with docs (use alongside Notion)
  • You need strong API access for integrations
  • You prioritize cost efficiency ($20/user is your ceiling)
  • You're building something that doesn't fit a standard template

When to Choose Smartsheet

Choose Smartsheet if:

  • You need native Gantt charts for project management
  • You're managing complex task dependencies and timelines
  • You need built-in resource capacity planning
  • Your team is familiar with Excel-like grid workflows
  • You have regulatory or compliance requirements (enterprise security)
  • You need portfolio-level reporting across multiple projects

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Airtable cheaper than Smartsheet?

For most small teams, yes. Airtable Collaborator is $20/user/month ($1,200/year for 5 users). Smartsheet Pro is $14/user/month, but most teams need the Business plan ($25/user = $1,500/year). For flexibility and price, Airtable usually wins for teams under 10 users.

Can I migrate from Smartsheet to Airtable?

Yes, but it's not simple. Smartsheet's task dependencies don't have a direct Airtable equivalent — you'll need to redesign workflows. Use tools like Zapier or manual export/import to move data. Plan for 1-2 weeks of setup time for complex projects.

Which tool is better for project management?

Smartsheet wins for traditional project management. It has native Gantt charts, resource capacity planning, and dependency management. Airtable is better for flexible, custom workflows that don't fit a project template.

Can I use Airtable instead of Smartsheet?

It depends on your needs. If you need Gantt charts and timelines, no — Airtable doesn't have these natively. If you need a flexible database with custom views and automations, yes — Airtable is much better.

Why is Smartsheet more expensive?

Smartsheet targets enterprise project management. The pricing reflects Gantt charts, resource management, portfolio reporting, and white-label options. These are valuable for large organizations managing multiple projects, but smaller teams don't need them.

Which has a better free tier?

Airtable's free tier (1,000 records/base) is much more generous than Smartsheet (200 rows). If you're testing, Airtable is worth trying first.
