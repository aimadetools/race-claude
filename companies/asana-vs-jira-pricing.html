Who Should Choose Asana?

  • Cross-functional teams — Marketing, design, product, and ops collaborating on shared projects
  • Complex workflows — Teams needing advanced timeline/Gantt chart views
  • Non-technical teams — Easier learning curve than Jira for traditional project management
  • Enterprise requirements — More flexible permission/approval structures

Who Should Choose Jira?

  • Software development teams — Built by and for engineers; native Agile/Scrum
  • GitHub integrations — Seamless developer workflow with auto-linking, pull requests
  • Budget-conscious dev shops — 34% cheaper than Asana for comparable features
  • Complex issue tracking — Sophisticated workflows, custom fields, link types
  • Time tracking & reporting — Integrated burndown charts, velocity calculations

Pricing History

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Asana or Jira better for non-technical teams?

Asana is more intuitive for non-technical teams. Jira's terminology (stories, epics, sprints, velocity) is Agile-specific. However, Jira has improved its UI significantly and works fine for any team with some onboarding.

Can I run both Asana and Jira at the same time?

Yes, many larger organizations use Asana for general project/product management and Jira for engineering work. Both have integrations that allow information flow between them, though this requires automation setup.

Which tool has the steepest learning curve?

Jira is more complex initially (more customization options, developer-oriented terminology), but Asana has more views to learn (board, timeline, list, calendar, etc.). For 1:1 training time, Asana is faster to onboard.

What about Jira Server vs Cloud?

Atlassian discontinued Server in Feb 2024. Everyone must migrate to Cloud. If you're on-premise, Data Center is the only option (enterprise-only, $35K+/year starting price). Cloud pricing shown here applies to all new customers.

Can I negotiate volume pricing?

Both offer discounts at scale (typically 10+ users), but less aggressively than smaller tools. Reach out directly for enterprise quotes.

Which is cheaper for a startup of 8 people?

Jira: $691.20/year (Standard plan) | Asana: $1,051.20/year (Team plan). Jira saves you ~$360/year at this size.
