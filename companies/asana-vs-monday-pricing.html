Which Should You Choose?

Choose Asana if: You need portfolio management and resource planning. You want native time tracking and capacity planning. You're managing complex, multi-team projects. You need advanced reporting.

Choose Monday.com if: You're budget-conscious and want the lowest cost option. You prefer simplicity and ease of setup. You want unlimited automations on all tiers. Your team isn't doing resource planning.

The truth: Asana is more expensive but more powerful for enterprise project management. Monday.com is the budget-friendly alternative for simpler use cases.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Asana or Monday.com cheaper?

Monday.com is significantly cheaper. A 10-person team pays ~$100/month ($1,200/year) on Monday.com Basic vs $109.90/month (~$1,319/year) on Asana Starter. Monday.com is about the same price but with less feature.

Did Asana raise prices in 2026?

Yes. In January 2026, Asana increased all plan prices. Starter went from $9 to $10.99, Standard from $15 to $18, and Advanced from $20 to $24.99. This was one of several price increases since 2024.

Which has better portfolio management?

Asana has native portfolio management and resource capacity planning. Monday.com doesn't have these features. If you're managing multiple projects across teams, Asana is better.

Does Monday.com have time tracking?Monday.com has limited time tracking features. Asana's time tracking is more robust and integrated. If time tracking is critical, Asana is the better choice.

Which is easier to learn?

Monday.com is generally easier to learn and set up. Asana is more powerful but has a steeper learning curve. For teams without project management experience, Monday.com is simpler.
