When to Choose Each Platform

Choose Asana If:

  • Product/project teams need timelines
  • Portfolio/multi-project management critical
  • Team has strong non-technical users
  • Resource management is essential
  • Prefer polished, professional interface
  • Budget: $1,619/year for 10 people

Choose Monday.com If:

  • Visual workflows and automation matter
  • Creative/marketing teams using it
  • Need advanced dashboards and reporting
  • Flexible project structures required
  • Free tier: 2 projects, unlimited users
  • Budget: $1,440/year for 10 people

Choose Notion If:

  • Need all-in-one workspace flexibility
  • Knowledge base + tasks + docs needed
  • Team values customization highly
  • Budget is paramount concern
  • Want minimal dependencies/integrations
  • Budget: $1,200/year for 10 people (cheapest)

💰 Cost Verdict

Cheapest for 10 people: Notion at $1,200/year ($10/user/mo)

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I migrate from Asana to Notion or Monday?

Yes. Asana has export tools, and third-party services can help. Monday and Notion both support CSV imports. Plan 2-3 weeks for a team migration. Backups of completed projects are wise first.

Which is best for non-technical teams?

Asana is easiest for non-technical users (polished UI, tutorials). Monday is very intuitive for visual workflows. Notion has a steeper learning curve but is rewarding for power users.

Which integrates best with Slack/Teams?

All three integrate well. Monday.com has the most powerful native Slack integration with full workflow automation. Asana and Notion both offer good Slack apps for notifications and quick task creation.

Does pricing include annual discounts?

Asana: ~10% annual discount. Monday: ~20% annual discount. Notion: ~15% annual discount. Annual billing is typically cheaper across all three.

Which is best for Agile/Scrum teams?

Monday.com has the best native Agile/sprint views. Asana supports sprint workflows well. Notion requires custom database setups for Agile but is doable if you invest time.
