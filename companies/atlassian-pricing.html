Atlassian Price Increase History

Cloud Price Increase — ~15% All Products October 2025
Atlassian raised cloud pricing approximately 15% across Jira Software, Confluence, Jira Service Management, and most other cloud products. This was announced in August 2025, giving teams 60 days to prepare. Atlassian cited increased infrastructure costs and AI feature investments (Atlassian Intelligence).
Jira Standard: $7.75/user/mo→$8.15/user/mo
Confluence Standard: $4.89/user/mo→$5.16/user/mo
Server Product End-of-Life — Forced Cloud Migration February 2024
Atlassian ended support for all Server products (Jira Server, Confluence Server). Teams were forced to choose between cloud migration or Atlassian's Data Center licensing. For most SMBs, cloud was the only practical option — but cloud pricing is per-user/month vs. the old server model which was a one-time (annual) license fee. This effectively raised costs for many teams by 40–200%.
Cloud Price Increase — ~10% Across Products April 2023
First major cloud price increase after the server sunset announcement. Jira Standard went from $7.50 to $7.75/user/month. Atlassian justified this with new features and infrastructure improvements. Many customers were still reeling from server-to-cloud cost shock.
Jira Standard: $7.50/user/mo→$7.75/user/mo
Cloud Strategy Launched — Long-term Pricing Stability Promised 2021
Atlassian announced the server sunset and pushed the "cloud-first" strategy. At the time, they promised 3 years of pricing predictability on cloud. That promise expired before the 2023 and 2025 increases.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Jira free plan good enough for a small development team?
For teams under 10 people, yes — Jira Free is genuinely capable. You get Scrum and Kanban boards, backlog, basic roadmaps, and integration with GitHub/Bitbucket. The key limitation is the 10-user cap and 2GB storage. Once you hit 11 users, you're forced to Standard at $8.15/user/month for everyone. Plan your growth accordingly — many teams hit this wall unexpectedly.
What's the difference between Jira and Trello — and which should I use?
Jira is built for software development with Agile methodology — sprints, story points, epics, backlogs, burndown charts. It has a steeper learning curve but is the industry standard for engineering teams. Trello is a simpler visual Kanban board suitable for non-technical project management, marketing campaigns, personal organization. If you have developers running sprints, use Jira. If you have a marketing team managing content, use Trello. They serve fundamentally different use cases despite both being "project management."
Can I get a discount by bundling Jira + Confluence?
Atlassian doesn't offer a formal bundle discount for Jira + Confluence on cloud. Both are billed separately per user. However, they do offer volume discounts at higher user tiers — pricing decreases on a per-user basis as you add more users (priced in "tiers": 1–10, 11–100, 101–300, etc.). Enterprise pricing at 801+ users includes additional discounts and flexibility.
Is Atlassian planning more price increases?
Atlassian hasn't announced future increases, but the pattern is clear: two cloud price hikes in 2023 and 2025, justified by infrastructure and AI investment. With "Atlassian Intelligence" features expanding, additional premium tier pricing or AI add-ons are possible. PricePulse monitors Atlassian's pricing pages — sign up free to get alerts when things change.
What happened to Atlassian Server and Data Center pricing?
Atlassian ended Server licensing in February 2024. Data Center licenses are still available for on-premise deployments but are priced significantly higher than the old Server licenses. Data Center Jira Software starts at $42,000/year for up to 500 users — designed for large enterprises with compliance requirements, not SMBs. Most teams that used Server have migrated to cloud or adopted alternatives.
