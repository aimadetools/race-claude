When to Choose Canva
  • Non-designers need quick, beautiful designs (social media, marketing materials)
  • You need pre-made templates and design suggestions (Canva has 5,000+)
  • Budget is tight — Pro at $15/mo is cheaper per seat than Figma teams
  • You need video editing or image background removal built-in
  • Small teams where designers work mostly solo with stakeholder feedback
When to Choose Figma
  • Professional UI/UX design with prototyping and developer handoff
  • You need component libraries and design systems (organization plan)
  • Team has many stakeholders/developers viewing (Figma's unlimited viewers save money)
  • Large teams — organization plan ($45/editor) becomes better at scale than Canva Teams
  • Complex workflows requiring version history and project organization

Pricing History

Canva

  • August 2024: Pro raised +15% from $12.99/mo to $15/mo. Teams plan introduced at $10/seat.
  • Before 2024: Pro had been $12.99/mo since 2021.
  • Trend: Canva's primary monetization is per-seat pricing for teams + Teams plan growth.

Figma

  • 2024–2026: Professional plan stable at $12/editor/mo (annual), ~$14.40/mo (monthly).
  • Organization plan: Introduced for mid-market, $45/editor/mo.
  • Trend: Figma focuses on expanding upmarket with design systems (Organization tier).

Money-Saving Tips

  • Canva: Annual plan saves 20% vs monthly ($119/yr vs $180/yr). For teams, negotiate enterprise pricing if 10+ seats.
  • Figma: Annual billing saves ~20% vs monthly. Free tier can handle 3 small projects if you're not collaborating heavily.
  • For 5+ designers: Canva Teams ($10/seat) is cheaper upfront. But Figma's unlimited viewers can save money on stakeholder licenses.
  • If you need both: Hybrid approach — Canva for marketing/social, Figma for product design ($25–35/mo total per person).

FAQ

Is Canva good enough for professional design?
Canva is excellent for non-designers and marketing teams creating social media, presentations, and branded templates. For UI/UX design, app mockups, or design systems, Figma is the professional standard. Canva lacks prototyping, component libraries, and developer handoff.
Does Figma free tier work for small teams?
Figma free tier limits you to 3 projects and 2 editors, but unlimited viewers. It can work for 2-person design teams working on separate projects. For more editors or active collaboration, Professional plan ($12/editor/mo) is required.
Can I import Canva designs into Figma?
Partially. Canva exports as PNG/PDF/SVG. Figma can import SVG and images, but you lose layer structure. Design recreation is usually faster than import.
Which is cheaper for a 10-person design team?
Canva Teams: 10 × $10/mo = $100/mo. Figma Organization: 10 × $45/mo = $450/mo. For pure cost, Canva wins. But if designers use Figma's design systems, prototyping, and stakeholders view designs (unlimited on Figma), Figma becomes competitive.
Does Canva have video editing?
Yes, Canva Pro and Teams include video editing. Figma does not. If your team needs quick video editing, Canva's all-in-one approach saves money vs buying separate tools.
Which should a startup use?
Early-stage startup with 1–2 designers: Figma Professional ($12/mo) for product design + Canva Free for marketing. As you scale, add Figma viewers for devs/stakeholders (free) and use Canva Teams ($10/seat) for marketing team.
