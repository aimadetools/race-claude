ClickUp Pricing History (2024–2026)

ClickUp built its user base partly on aggressive pricing, then raised prices sharply once established. Here's the full record:

Unlimited plan up 43%, Business up 58% February 2026
ClickUp's largest-ever price increase. The Unlimited plan — ClickUp's most popular tier — jumped from $7 to $10/seat. Business went from $12 to $19/seat, a 58% increase. Teams on annual subscriptions saw the new rate applied at their next renewal date without advance email notice.
Unlimited (annual):$7/seat→$10/seat
Business (annual):$12/seat→$19/seat
Free plan: automation and dashboard limits added Q3 2025
ClickUp added limits to its Free Forever plan: automations capped at 100/month (previously unlimited) and Dashboards limited to 1 per workspace. Teams using ClickUp's automation features extensively were pushed to Unlimited or Business.
Free automations:Unlimited→100/month
Free dashboards:Unlimited→1
ClickUp AI added as paid add-on Q1 2025
ClickUp launched ClickUp Brain (AI writing assistant, task summarization, automated standup reports) as a $7/member/month add-on across all plans including Free. This was later folded into Business plan pricing during the February 2026 increase.
ClickUp Brain:Not available→$7/member/mo add-on
Business Plus plan discontinued Q4 2024
ClickUp removed its Business Plus tier ($19/seat), collapsing the lineup from 5 tiers to 4. Existing Business Plus customers were migrated to Business at a discounted rate for 12 months. After the migration period, they renewed at the new Business pricing.
Tiers:Free, Unlimited, Business, Business Plus, Enterprise→Free, Unlimited, Business, Enterprise

What ClickUp's Price Surge Means for Competitors

ClickUp was founded on being the "affordable alternative" to Asana and Monday. The February 2026 price increase eliminated that positioning. Unlimited at $10/seat is now comparable to Asana Starter and Monday Standard — tools that were previously considered "premium" alternatives.

Teams who chose ClickUp specifically for cost reasons are now re-evaluating. Alternatives like Linear (for engineering teams), Notion (for knowledge workers), and Monday.com are all running campaigns targeting ClickUp's newly price-sensitive customers.

The timing is significant: ClickUp's price increase coincided with Monday's February 2026 increase, creating a rare moment where multiple major project management tools raised prices simultaneously.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is ClickUp's Free plan actually unlimited?

ClickUp's Free Forever plan includes unlimited tasks and unlimited members, which is genuinely more generous than many competitors. However, since Q3 2025, it's limited to 100MB storage, 1 dashboard, 100 automations/month, and no time tracking or goals. For most solo users it's sufficient; teams need Unlimited or Business.

What did ClickUp Unlimited cost before the 2026 price increase?

Unlimited was $7/seat/month (annual) before February 2026. It's now $10/seat — a 43% increase. If you were previously paying $84/seat/year, you're now paying $120/seat/year. For a 20-person team, that's an extra $720/year.

Does ClickUp offer a nonprofit or education discount?

ClickUp offers free accounts for qualifying nonprofits and educational institutions. Apply through their website with proof of nonprofit or .edu status. Startups may also qualify for startup program pricing through partner accelerators.

How does ClickUp Business compare to Monday Pro?

Both are now priced at $19/seat/month (annual). ClickUp Business includes unlimited automations, advanced dashboards, and time tracking. Monday Pro includes private boards, Gantt charts, and Monday AI. The best choice depends on your workflow — ClickUp offers more customization; Monday offers a more structured interface that some teams find easier to standardize.

Will ClickUp raise prices again?

Unknown, but ClickUp has raised prices twice in 18 months. The best way to stay informed is to monitor their pricing page directly — PricePulse can alert you within hours of any change.
