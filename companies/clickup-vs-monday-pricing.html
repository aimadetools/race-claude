Which Should You Choose?

Choose ClickUp if: You want a more flexible, customizable project management tool. You need time tracking built-in. You prefer a more generous free tier. You want extensive documentation features.

Choose Monday.com if: You want simplicity and ease of use out of the box. You need unlimited automations even on lower tiers. You want a highly visual, no-code workflow builder. Your team isn't tech-savvy.

The real difference: ClickUp is more customizable and feature-rich; Monday.com is simpler and more beginner-friendly. Both are strong at project management.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is cheaper: ClickUp or Monday.com?

For small teams with annual billing, ClickUp is slightly cheaper. A 10-person team paying $50/year on ClickUp vs $80/year on Monday.com. However, pricing changes frequently — monitor both with PricePulse alerts.

Did ClickUp or Monday.com raise prices in 2026?

Yes. ClickUp increased prices significantly in February 2026 (Unlimited went from $7 to $10, then to $19). Monday.com raised prices in February 2026 (Basic and Standard increased). Both are now in the $8–19/seat range.

Which has a better free plan?

ClickUp's free plan is more generous. It offers unlimited users, unlimited spaces, and core features. Monday.com's free plan is limited to 5 seats and basic features. If you have a small team on a budget, ClickUp wins.

Does ClickUp have automations?

Yes, but they're limited on free/lower tiers. Monday.com offers unlimited automations on all paid plans, which is an advantage if you heavily use workflow automation.

Is Monday.com easier to use than ClickUp?

Generally yes. Monday.com is known for being more intuitive and beginner-friendly. ClickUp is more powerful but has a steeper learning curve. If ease of use is critical, Monday.com is the better choice.
