Which Should You Choose?

Choose ClickUp if: Project management is your primary use case. You need Gantt charts, time tracking, sprints, and resource management. Your team manages complex multi-project workflows. You need 15+ view types to visualize work differently.

Choose Notion if: Docs and knowledge management are equally important as tasks. You want an all-in-one workspace where tasks, wikis, and databases live together. You prioritize ease of use over feature depth. You're sensitive to the 43% ClickUp price increase and need a more affordable option.

Key 2026 insight: ClickUp's 58% price hike on the Business plan (from $12 to $19/user/mo) has pushed many teams to evaluate Notion as a lower-cost alternative. For teams where documentation and knowledge management matter as much as task tracking, Notion is now clearly cheaper and arguably more flexible.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is ClickUp or Notion cheaper?

Notion is cheaper. Notion Plus at $8/user/mo vs ClickUp Business at $19/user/mo. For 10 users annually, Notion saves $1,320/year. ClickUp's Unlimited plan ($7/user/mo) is comparable in price to Notion Plus, but lacks many of the features teams typically need.

Why did ClickUp raise prices in 2026?

ClickUp cited AI feature additions (ClickUp Brain) as the primary reason for the February 2026 price hike. The Business plan increased from $12 to $19/user/mo — a 58% increase. Teams can opt out of AI features but cannot get the pre-hike pricing.

Can Notion replace ClickUp for project management?

For many teams, yes. Notion Projects (launched 2023) added timeline views, sprints, and project management features. The main gaps vs ClickUp: no native time tracking, fewer view types, and less granular task automations. For engineering or product teams needing deep PM features, ClickUp may still be worth the premium.

Which has a better free plan?

Both have strong free plans. ClickUp Free offers unlimited tasks across unlimited projects, which is excellent for small teams. Notion Free offers unlimited pages/blocks but limits workspace collaboration (7-day history, no unlimited guests). For task-heavy teams on a zero budget, ClickUp's free plan is more powerful.
