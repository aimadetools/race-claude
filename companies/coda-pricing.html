Understanding Coda's Pricing Model

Key difference from Notion: Coda charges per workspace, not per user. One Team plan ($20/mo) covers up to 20 team members.

What You Get at Each Tier

Free 2 docs max, email only
Team ($20/mo) Unlimited docs, up to 20 users included
Pro ($30/mo) Unlimited docs, unlimited users, automation, integrations

Coda Team Plan Features

The Team plan ($20/mo) is Coda's most popular tier for workgroups:

  • Unlimited documents and folders
  • Up to 20 team members (included in price)
  • Advanced sharing and permissions (document-level and folder-level)
  • Integrations (Slack, Google Drive, Zapier)
  • Collaborative editing (real-time co-editing)
  • Comments and annotations on docs
  • Docs version history
  • Email notifications and digests
  • Custom domain for published docs (optional)

Coda Pro Plan Features

The Pro plan ($30/mo) adds advanced automation and unlimited users:

  • All Team features
  • Unlimited team members (no per-seat cost)
  • Advanced automation (Coda Automations, Pack actions)
  • Custom Packs (integrate third-party tools)
  • Advanced API access
  • Priority support
  • Batch file uploads
  • Advanced analytics and usage tracking

Coda vs Notion vs Evernote

How Coda's pricing compares to similar platforms:

Coda Team $20/mo — Per workspace (covers up to 20 users)
Notion Pro $10/mo — Per user (add-on members cost extra)
Evernote Premium $9.99/mo — Per user (note-taking only)

Key insight: Coda's workspace-based pricing favors teams with 5+ members. At 8 people, Coda is $2.50/person. Notion would cost more per person if you need to upgrade multiple users.

When to Upgrade Tiers

  • Free → Team ($20/mo): When you have 3+ docs and team members need shared access
  • Team → Pro ($30/mo): When you need automation, custom integrations, or more than 20 users
  • Pro → Enterprise: When you need SSO, 24/7 support, or compliance features (SOC 2, HIPAA)
