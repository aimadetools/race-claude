ConvertKit Pricing 2026

ConvertKit is an email marketing platform built for creators. It combines email hosting with landing pages, subscriber management, and visual automation—purpose-built for newsletters, bloggers, and content creators.

Quick Pricing Summary

  • Free: $0 (up to 1,000 subscribers, unlimited emails, no automations)
  • Creator: $25/month (up to 1,000 subscribers, landing pages, automations, audience tagging)
  • Creator Plus: $75/month (up to 10,000 subscribers, custom domains, advanced analytics, tags)
  • Creator Pro: $150/month (unlimited subscribers, premium templates, API access, priority support)
  • Best free tier:** 1,000 free subscribers (beats Mailchimp 500 and Brevo 300)
  • Best for:** Newsletter writers, bloggers, creators, solo entrepreneurs monetizing email

When to Choose ConvertKit

  • You're a creator or newsletter writer: ConvertKit is purpose-built for creators with subscriber tagging, sequences, and landing pages optimized for audience growth
  • You want the best free tier: 1,000 free subscribers (beats Mailchimp's 500 and Brevo's 300)
  • You're building a personal brand via email: Founder letters, newsletters, subscriber funnels—all baked into ConvertKit
  • Landing pages matter: ConvertKit's landing page builder is more powerful than Mailchimp's for creators
  • You're scaling beyond 1,000 subscribers: Creator Plus ($75/mo for 10K subs) is competitive with Mailchimp/Brevo and includes more creator tools

When to Choose Alternatives

  • You need SMS marketing: Brevo ($20/mo) includes SMS; ConvertKit doesn't have SMS
  • You're a small business (not creator): Mailchimp ($20/mo) is more geared toward SMBs; ConvertKit feels overkill
  • You need transactional email: Brevo includes transactional (order confirmations, etc); ConvertKit doesn't
  • You want the absolute cheapest option: Mailchimp/Brevo at $20/mo (ConvertKit is $25/mo at entry)

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between ConvertKit's Free and Creator plan?

Free plan: broadcasts only (no email sequences/automations). Creator plan ($25/mo): full automation, landing pages, subscriber tagging. Both support up to 1,000 subscribers. The $25/mo Creator plan unlocks the automation that makes newsletters valuable.

Can I use ConvertKit for my e-commerce store?

ConvertKit is optimized for creators and newsletters, not e-commerce. If you need transactional emails (order confirmations), SMS marketing, or complex customer data, use Brevo or Klaviyo instead. ConvertKit is best for audience-first businesses (newsletters, courses, membership sites).

How is ConvertKit's email deliverability?

ConvertKit has strong deliverability (95%+) and IP warming practices for new accounts. It handles high-volume newsletters well. No complaints about spam folder issues if you follow email best practices.

Does ConvertKit offer annual discounts?

No annual billing discounts are currently offered. ConvertKit only bills monthly. Creator at $25/mo = $300/year (no discount for annual prepayment).

Can I import my list from Substack or Mailchimp?

Yes. ConvertKit supports CSV imports from Substack, Mailchimp, and any platform. You can import subscribers with their email, name, and custom fields. Migration usually takes 1-2 weeks including setting up automation.
