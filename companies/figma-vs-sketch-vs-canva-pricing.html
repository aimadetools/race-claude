When to Choose Each

Choose Figma If:

  • You're building a SaaS product — Figma excels at UX/UI design for web apps, with live prototyping and developer handoff
  • Your team needs real-time collaboration — See live cursors, comments, and edits happening simultaneously
  • You're establishing a design system — Figma's components, variants, and design tokens are industry-leading for large teams
  • You work cross-platform — Windows, Mac, Linux, iPad, or web—all fully featured
  • You hand off to developers frequently — Figma Inspect mode + generated code snippets streamline handoff

Choose Sketch If:

  • You're a solo Mac designer — $120/year is one of the cheapest options and has a mature ecosystem
  • You prefer offline, native apps — Sketch runs locally without internet (vs Figma's cloud dependency)
  • You need the most refined Mac experience — 10+ years of optimization for macOS UI conventions
  • Your team is all Mac users — Works seamlessly with Apple's design ecosystem
  • You value a massive plugin ecosystem — Sketch plugins solve almost any design problem

Choose Canva If:

  • You're non-technical / new to design — Canva's templates make professional design accessible to everyone
  • You create marketing materials — Social media graphics, flyers, presentations, posters, infographics
  • You need brand consistency at scale — Brand kit applies logos, colors, fonts across all team designs instantly
  • You want the cheapest team option — $180/person/year beats Figma or Sketch for small teams doing template-based work
  • Speed matters more than customization — Start with a template, customize in minutes vs hours in Figma
  • You work on mobile frequently — Canva's mobile apps are full-featured (Figma/Sketch are limited on mobile)

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use Canva for professional UI/UX design?

Not really. Canva is built for marketing materials, not complex app design. It lacks components, prototyping, and design tokens. Use Figma or Sketch for UI/UX work.

Can I switch from Figma to Canva or vice versa?

Switching from Figma → Canva: Canva can't import Figma files, but you can export as PNG/SVG and rebuild in Canva (for templates). Switching from Canva → Figma: Export Canva designs as PNG/PDF, then manually recreate in Figma (no direct import).

Does Canva have a free tier?

Yes. Canva Free includes 5M+ templates and basic features. Most users upgrade to Canva Pro ($120/yr) for unlimited templates and storage. Teams need Canva Teams ($180/person/yr).

Which tool scales best for enterprise?

Figma. For 50+ person orgs, Figma's SSO, permissions, design tokens, and component libraries are unmatched. Sketch is mid-market. Canva is best for marketing departments, not engineering teams.

Can I use multiple tools in the same workflow?

Yes, but it's messy. Some teams use Figma for UI/UX and Canva for marketing materials (different purposes). Mix-and-match requires exporting/re-importing, which is slow.

What if my team is mixed (Mac + Windows)?

Figma or Canva. Sketch is Mac-only and won't work for Windows users. Figma is better for design work; Canva is better for marketing/template work.
