Google Workspace Price History

Google Apps / Workspace maintained flat pricing from 2006 until 2022 — a remarkable 17-year run. That ended with a major restructuring.

Business Starter: $6 → $7.20/user/month (+20%)
Business Standard: $12 → $14.40/user/month (+20%)
Business Plus: $18 → $21.60/user/month (+20%)
Google cited "investment in new features and AI capabilities" as justification.
Mar 2022
Rebranding + Restructure
Google Workspace plans restructured, new names introduced
Google rebranded G Suite → Google Workspace and restructured plan names (Business Starter, Standard, Plus). Prices remained the same during the rebrand — the increase came 9 months later in January 2023.
Mar 2022
Free Tier Eliminated
Google ends free legacy G Suite for businesses
Google eliminated the free tier of G Suite (grandfathered since 2012) forcing millions of small businesses and nonprofits to either pay or migrate to personal Gmail. This was widely seen as a test of price sensitivity before the 2023 commercial increase.
2024
AI Add-On Launch
Gemini for Workspace launches as paid add-on
Google launched Gemini Business ($10/user/month) and Gemini Enterprise ($20/user/month) as add-ons. Unlike Microsoft's Copilot ($30/user), Gemini is included in higher-tier Enterprise plans and priced more competitively for SMBs.
2006–2022
17 Years Flat
No price increases for 17 years
Google Apps launched in 2006 at $50/user/year. The product was renamed G Suite, then Google Workspace, features were added, storage grew — but the price stayed flat for nearly two decades.

Is Google Workspace Worth the Price?

When Google Workspace is the clear choice

  • Cloud-first, browser-based teams: If your work is in tabs, Workspace is faster and more collaborative than Office. Real-time co-authoring still beats M365 for quick edits.
  • Startups and new businesses: Workspace is easier to set up, manage, and scale than M365. Google's admin console is significantly simpler than Azure AD.
  • Mac/Linux/cross-platform teams: Workspace runs identically on every device. No worrying about Windows-only features.
  • Cost-sensitive teams needing AI: Gemini at $10/user vs Copilot at $30/user — same category, 3× cheaper.
  • Remote-first companies: Google Meet, shared drives, and real-time docs are purpose-built for distributed teams.

When Microsoft 365 is probably better

  • You need Excel's full power: Complex financial models, advanced macros, Power Query, pivot tables — Google Sheets still lags behind.
  • Existing Microsoft infrastructure: Active Directory, Azure, Intune — the integration cost of switching is high.
  • Regulated industries: Some compliance frameworks specifically require M365 tools (e.g., certain government contracts require Microsoft-hosted data).
  • Windows device management: Microsoft Intune (Business Premium) is native. Managing Windows fleets in Workspace requires third-party tools.

Frequently Asked Questions

What happened to the free Google Workspace plan?
In March 2022, Google eliminated the free legacy G Suite tier that had been grandfathered since 2012. Small businesses and individuals using G Suite for free were required to upgrade to a paid Workspace plan or switch to personal Google accounts. Google Workspace for Education and Nonprofits still have free tiers.
Is Business Starter enough for a small team?
For most small teams (under 10 people), yes — if you don't need video call recordings, large meeting rooms, or eDiscovery features. The main limitations are 30GB pooled storage (can fill up if team shares large files) and 100-person Meet cap. Upgrade to Standard ($14.40) when you need recordings or more storage.
How does Google Workspace storage work?
Storage is pooled across your organization (not per-user). Business Starter gets 30GB shared across all users — so a 10-person team shares 30GB total. Business Standard gets 2TB pooled storage (much more reasonable for 10+ users). Storage is shared across Gmail, Drive, and Google Photos.
Is Gemini worth the extra $10/user/month?
It depends on your use case. Gemini in Gmail (smart replies, email summarization) and Docs (writing assistance, summarization) adds real value for knowledge workers who write a lot. For teams doing mostly spreadsheet work or video calls, the AI features may feel redundant. Consider trialing with power users first before rolling out to all seats.
Will Google raise Workspace prices again?
Likely. The 2023 increase was framed as a one-time correction, but Google's ongoing investment in Gemini AI requires cost recovery. We monitor Google Workspace pricing pages 24/7. Sign up free to get notified the moment prices change.
Can I switch from M365 to Google Workspace?
Yes, but migration is non-trivial. Google provides a free migration tool (Google Workspace Migration for Microsoft Exchange/Outlook). Email migrates well; shared calendars are mostly fine; complex Excel files with macros or Power Query may need rework. Budget 2-4 weeks for a 20-person team migration.
