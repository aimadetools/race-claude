Free, Premium & Business

Grammarly Premium costs $12/month billed annually ($144/year). But the AI writing landscape has shifted dramatically — here's what you actually get vs. what free tools now offer.

⚠️
Context shift (2024–2026): ChatGPT, Claude, and Microsoft Copilot now offer sophisticated grammar checking and rewriting for free or at lower cost than Grammarly Premium. Grammarly has responded by accelerating its own AI features — but pricing pressure is real.

Grammarly Price Increase History

Grammarly has been relatively measured with price increases but has restructured plans to limit the free tier while adding AI features to justify Premium costs.

Monthly Billing Price Raised — Free Tier AI Capped 2024
Grammarly capped the number of AI-assisted prompts in the free tier, effectively pushing AI writing features behind the Premium paywall. Monthly billing was also raised significantly — the gap between annual ($12/mo) and monthly ($30/mo) billing widened to 150%, creating pressure to commit annually.
Monthly: ~$25/mo→Monthly: $30/mo
Grammarly AI Launch — Features Moved Behind Paywall 2023
Grammarly launched "Grammarly AI" (generative rewriting, tone transformation, drafting) as a Premium/Business-only feature. Free tier users saw their access to AI suggestions capped or removed. This was framed as adding value to Premium rather than removing features from Free — but the practical effect was the same.
Business Plan Introduced — Team Pricing Added 2021
Grammarly introduced Business at $12.50/user/month (later raised to $15/user). The new tier was priced above Premium to capture team contracts, with style guides and analytics as the primary differentiators.
Business: $12.50/user/mo→Business: $15/user/mo
Premium Annual Price Stable 2018–Present
Grammarly Premium annual billing has stayed at $11.66–$12/month for several years. The company has chosen to devalue the free tier and raise monthly billing rather than touch the attractive annual rate — a classic SaaS "anchor pricing" strategy.

Is Grammarly Premium Worth It in 2026?

This is the honest question most people are asking. The answer depends heavily on your use case:

Worth it for:

  • Non-technical writers in high-stakes roles — Lawyers, executives, marketers who write daily and need reliable, discreet grammar checking built into their workflow (not "paste into ChatGPT")
  • ESL professionals — Grammar and tone suggestions trained on millions of English documents is Grammarly's core strength
  • Teams that need brand consistency — Business tier style guides are genuinely valuable for agencies and content teams
  • Anyone who needs plagiarism detection — Academic writers, journalists, content teams

Hard to justify at $144/year when:

  • You already pay for Microsoft 365 — Microsoft Editor (included in M365) covers grammar, clarity, and conciseness at zero extra cost
  • You use Claude or ChatGPT regularly — AI writing assistants now handle rewrites, tone adjustment, and proofreading on demand — often better than Grammarly
  • You're a technical writer — Technical jargon often gets incorrectly flagged; specialized tools or manual proofreading may serve you better
  • You mostly write in non-English languages — Grammarly's non-English support is basic; DeepL Write is better for EU languages

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the cheapest way to get Grammarly Premium?
Annual billing at $144/year ($12/month) is the lowest available rate. Grammarly occasionally offers discount codes via their own promotions — Black Friday deals have gone to 50% off ($6/month annually). Monthly billing at $30/month is 150% more expensive — avoid it unless you only need it for a specific project. Educational discounts are available for students through some university partnerships.
Is Grammarly Business worth it vs each person buying Premium?
At 3+ users: Premium costs $12/user/month ($36/month total for 3), Business costs $15/user/month ($45/month). Business adds $9/month for style guides, analytics, and team management. That's worth it for agencies or content teams where brand consistency matters — not worth it for a small team that just needs spell-check. Calculate your break-even based on how much time inconsistent brand voice costs your team.
Does Grammarly work with Microsoft Word?
Yes. Grammarly has a Microsoft Word add-in (Windows and Mac) that works in the document itself. This is one of Grammarly's genuine strengths — seamless integration vs. copying and pasting into a web tool. The Word integration requires a Grammarly account (free or premium).
Can I cancel Grammarly and get a refund?
Grammarly's refund policy allows refunds within 10 days of the start of a new billing period if you haven't substantially used the service. Annual subscribers can request a prorated refund in some cases — contact their support. This is more lenient than many SaaS tools but not unconditional. Check the terms at time of purchase.
Will Grammarly raise prices again?
The pressure is real. Grammarly is competing with free AI tools while investors expect growth. The most likely move is restricting the free tier further or adding a new higher-priced tier with advanced AI features, rather than raising Premium's annual price (which has been their customer acquisition anchor).
