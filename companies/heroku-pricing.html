Why Heroku Was (and Still Is) Popular

  • Deploy simplicity: Push to Heroku, auto-scales within dyno type
  • Built-in databases: Postgres, MySQL add-ons included in ecosystem
  • Git integration: Deploy directly from GitHub
  • Add-ons marketplace: Auth0, SendGrid, Stripe, etc. integrations pre-configured
  • No infrastructure management: No servers to configure, update, or maintain

Why Developers Are Leaving Heroku

Pricing shock: Free tier removal in Nov 2022 forced thousands to migrate overnight. A $0 app suddenly required $7+/month commitment.

  • Expensive for small apps: $7 Eco tier + $9 PostgreSQL = $16/month minimum (vs. $2-5 on competitors)
  • Performance concerns: Community reports slower cold starts vs. newer alternatives (Render, Railway)
  • Better alternatives: Vercel (Next.js native), Railway (simpler pricing), Render (same simplicity, cheaper)

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Heroku have a free tier?

No. Heroku removed the free tier on November 28, 2022. The cheapest option now is the Eco tier at $7/month.

What happened to Heroku free dynos?

Salesforce (Heroku's parent company) discontinued free dynos to reduce costs and focus on paying customers. This migration pushed thousands of developers to Render, Railway, and Vercel.

Is Heroku still worth it?

Heroku is worth it if: (1) You need seamless Git deployment, (2) Your team values simplicity over cost, (3) You're running mature, production apps. Not worth it for hobby projects or MVPs.

What's cheaper than Heroku?

Render ($7/month starter with free tier), Railway (pay-as-you-go), Vercel (free for Next.js), and AWS (free tier + cheap EC2).
