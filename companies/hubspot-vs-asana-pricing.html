When to Choose Each

Choose HubSpot If:

  • You need sales/marketing automation — HubSpot is built for managing deals and leads
  • Your budget is limited — Starter at $50/mo is cheaper than a small Asana team
  • You're a service business — Customer relationship management (CRM) is core
  • You want email integration — HubSpot's email sync is a killer feature
  • Your team is 1-5 people — HubSpot Starter is unbeatable value

Choose Asana If:

  • You need dedicated project management — Asana is built for task tracking and timelines
  • Your team needs time tracking — HubSpot doesn't have this
  • You use Kanban or Gantt workflows — More visual than HubSpot's deal board
  • You have engineering/creative teams — Better for non-sales workflows
  • You want tight team collaboration — Asana's commenting/docs are cleaner

Best Practice: Run Both

  • Use HubSpot for sales/marketing — Your customer relationships and revenue pipeline
  • Use Asana for operations/delivery — Your projects, product, and team workflows
  • Total cost for 5 people: $650/mo (HubSpot $50 + Asana $55 Teams + Zapier $29 for integration)
  • Benefit: Best-in-class tool for each use case, not compromising on either

Frequently Asked Questions

Can Asana replace HubSpot?

No. Asana is a project management tool; HubSpot is a CRM. Asana doesn't do email integration, contact management, or pipeline tracking. They solve different problems.

Can HubSpot replace Asana?

Partially. HubSpot has a deals board, tasks, and email integration. But it lacks Asana's time tracking, Gantt charts, and dedicated project workflows. HubSpot's task feature is a lightweight alternative, not a replacement.

Which is cheaper?

For a 5-person team: HubSpot Starter ($50/mo) is cheaper than Asana Teams ($55/mo). But if you need project management features, you're comparing HubSpot ($50) + a lightweight PM tool vs Asana alone ($55). At 10+ people, HubSpot Pro ($500) becomes expensive; Asana stays per-user.

Do they integrate?

Yes. Use Zapier (paid) or HubSpot's native integrations. You can send HubSpot deals to Asana tasks, or vice versa. Not seamless, but workable.

Which has better customer support?

HubSpot has superior support (dedicated account manager on paid plans). Asana's support is good but more self-service. For HubSpot Pro, you get priority support.

What about onboarding?

HubSpot: Excellent. Free academy courses, detailed onboarding, lots of templates. Asana: Good. Lots of templates, but fewer native learning resources. HubSpot wins for first-time users.
