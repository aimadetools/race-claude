Which Should You Choose?

Choose HubSpot if: You want affordable CRM with marketing automation built-in. You're a startup or small business on a budget. You want to get started quickly with minimal setup. You prefer an all-in-one CRM platform.

Choose Salesforce if: You need deep customization and advanced functionality. You're an enterprise with complex sales processes. You need Service Cloud for customer support. You're willing to invest heavily in a CRM.

The reality: HubSpot is the clear choice for cost. Salesforce is the choice for power and scale.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is HubSpot or Salesforce cheaper?

HubSpot is dramatically cheaper. A 5-person team costs $225/month on HubSpot Starter vs $1,000/month on Salesforce Professional. HubSpot's affordability is its biggest advantage over Salesforce.

Did Salesforce raise prices in 2026?

Yes. In January 2026, Salesforce increased all plan prices. Essentials rose to $180/user (from $165), Professional to $200 (from $165), and Enterprise to $300+ (from $240). This was a significant increase across the board.

Can I use HubSpot for service/support management?

HubSpot has basic ticketing features, but it's not as powerful as Salesforce Service Cloud. If you need dedicated service management, Salesforce is better, though it costs more.

Does HubSpot have marketing automation?

Yes, and it's included in HubSpot's main CRM pricing. Salesforce requires a separate Marketing Cloud subscription, which adds significant cost. HubSpot's all-in-one approach is cheaper.

Which has better customization?

Salesforce is far more customizable and flexible. But HubSpot has improved customization features over time and is good enough for most small-to-medium businesses. Salesforce is the choice for deep custom development.

Should I use both?

If you need Salesforce's power but want to control costs, some companies use HubSpot for basic CRM and Salesforce for specific use cases. However, integration complexity makes this uncommon. It's usually an either/or choice.
