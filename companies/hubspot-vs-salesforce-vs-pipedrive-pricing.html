HubSpot CRM Pricing — All Plans (2026)

HubSpot's trap: the free CRM is genuinely good, but the moment you need sales sequences, advanced reporting, or automation, you're looking at Professional tier — $100/user/month. That's the most abrupt pricing cliff in the CRM market.

Annual billing required for most discounts. Monthly billing is ~20% more. HubSpot also sells Marketing Hub, Service Hub, and CMS Hub as separate products — the full suite can be significantly more expensive.

Which CRM Should You Choose?

Choose HubSpot if...

You need both CRM and marketing automation in one platform, your team is small and can use the free CRM tier effectively, or you're already using HubSpot Marketing Hub and want a unified system. Watch out for the cliff to Professional at $100/seat — budget for it before you need sequences.

Choose Salesforce if...

You need maximum customization (custom objects, complex workflows, advanced integrations), you're a 50+ person sales team with dedicated Salesforce admin resources, or your industry requires Salesforce-specific integrations (financial services, healthcare, manufacturing). Not for small teams without admin support.

Choose Pipedrive if...

Your primary use case is sales pipeline management (not marketing automation or complex customer service), you want predictable per-seat pricing without minimums or surprise tiers, or you're a 3–50 person sales team that doesn't need Salesforce's complexity. Best value for pure sales CRM use cases.

HubSpot vs Salesforce vs Pipedrive FAQ

Is HubSpot really free?
HubSpot's free CRM is genuinely free with no time limit — not a free trial. It includes unlimited users, 1M contacts, basic deal pipelines, meeting scheduling, and live chat. The free tier is legitimately useful for small teams. The catch is that key sales productivity features (email sequences, advanced reporting, forecasting) require Professional tier at $100/user/month.
Why is Salesforce so much more expensive than Pipedrive?
Salesforce is more expensive because it's a platform, not just a CRM. It's highly customizable, has thousands of ecosystem integrations, and is designed for complex enterprise sales processes with multiple teams, territories, and products. Pipedrive is purpose-built for managing a linear sales pipeline — simpler, cheaper, and easier to use. If you don't need Salesforce's complexity, you're paying for infrastructure you won't use.
Can I migrate from one CRM to another?
Yes, all three CRMs support CSV import/export. HubSpot and Pipedrive both have migration tools and partner agencies. Salesforce migrations are more complex due to custom objects and workflow logic. The bigger switching cost is typically re-training your team and rebuilding integrations, not the data migration itself.
