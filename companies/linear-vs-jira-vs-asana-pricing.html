When to Choose Each Platform

Choose Linear If:

  • You're a modern SaaS/engineering team
  • Speed and developer experience matter most
  • You prefer a lightweight, clean interface
  • Team is <50 people
  • You value API-first development
  • Budget: ~$1,200/year for 10 people

Choose Jira If:

  • Enterprise adoption/legacy systems required
  • Complex workflows and automation essential
  • You need advanced reporting and analytics
  • Team spans engineering + multiple departments
  • Portfolio management is critical
  • Budget: ~$900/year for 10 people (cheapest)

Choose Asana If:

  • Product or marketing teams using it
  • Non-technical stakeholders need full access
  • Timeline/Gantt charts essential for planning
  • Resource management across teams important
  • You want lowest barrier to entry
  • Budget: ~$1,619/year for 10 people

💰 Cost Verdict

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use Linear, Jira, and Asana together?

Yes! Many teams use Linear for engineering, Asana for product/marketing, and Jira for complex backend workflows. They integrate via webhooks and API. This adds cost but provides best-of-breed tooling.

Which has the best free tier?

Jira's free tier supports up to 10 users (most generous), Asana's free tier supports 15 team members, Linear's free tier is limited to 3 users. For testing, Jira or Asana's free tiers will let you evaluate longer.

Which is best for startups?

Linear is the startup favorite—fast, developer-friendly, simple pricing ($10/user/month, no hidden costs). Jira's free tier lets you test longer. Asana is better if non-engineers use it.

Does pricing include discounts for annual billing?

Linear: ~20% discount for annual. Jira: varies by plan, ~15-20% typical. Asana: ~20% discount. All three offer better rates for annual vs. monthly billing.

What about time tracking or reporting?

Jira and Asana have built-in time tracking. Linear has limited time tracking but excellent burndown charts. For detailed time reporting, Jira > Asana > Linear.
