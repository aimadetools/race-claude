Mailchimp Price Increase History (2021–2026)

Mailchimp was acquired by Intuit in November 2021 for $12 billion. The acquisition triggered a wave of pricing and plan restructuring that has significantly increased costs for small businesses.

Essentials Price Increase — +54% May 2024
Mailchimp raised the Essentials plan entry price from $13/month to $20/month for 500 contacts. Standard also increased from $20 to $35/month. Existing users received 60 days notice but no grandfathering. The change followed an earlier increase in 2023.
$13/month (Essentials, 500 contacts)→$20/month
$20/month (Standard, 500 contacts)→$35/month
Mid-Tier Price Restructuring April 2023
Mailchimp restructured its audience-based pricing, effectively raising costs for users with 500–2,500 contacts. The Essentials plan went from $9.99 to $13/month for 500 contacts. Standard from $14.99 to $20/month. Premium (previously $299) reorganized to scale more aggressively.
$9.99/month (Essentials, 500 contacts)→$13/month
Free Plan Restricted Post-Acquisition February 2022
Mailchimp reduced the free plan from 2,000 contacts to 500 contacts, and from 10,000 monthly sends to 1,000. This effectively forced growing accounts into paid plans much earlier. Many small businesses discovered their accounts suddenly required payment when they had expected free access.
Free: 2,000 contacts / 10,000 sends→500 contacts / 1,000 sends
Pre-Acquisition Pricing (Stable) 2001–2021
Before the Intuit acquisition, Mailchimp was known for fair, founder-friendly pricing. The free plan supported 2,000 contacts, and paid plans scaled reasonably. The company was also employee-owned with a reputation for not over-monetizing. The Intuit acquisition fundamentally changed this trajectory.

Mailchimp Pricing FAQ

Why did Mailchimp become so expensive?
Mailchimp was acquired by Intuit (makers of QuickBooks and TurboTax) in November 2021 for $12 billion. As a public company, Intuit faces pressure to grow Mailchimp's revenue. This has resulted in annual price increases, free plan restrictions, and plan restructuring — all of which have been controversial in the small business community.
Does Mailchimp's free plan still exist?
Yes, but it's much more restrictive than it used to be. The free plan now supports 500 contacts and 1,000 monthly email sends (reduced from 2,000 contacts and 10,000 sends in 2022). It also includes Mailchimp branding on all emails. For businesses growing beyond 500 contacts, the free plan quickly becomes limiting.
Is Mailchimp worth the price compared to alternatives?
For most growing businesses, no — especially at scale. Brevo offers similar features at 3–5× lower cost on a send-volume model. For e-commerce, Klaviyo delivers better ROI despite similar or higher pricing. For creators and bloggers, Kit (ConvertKit) has better UX for sequence-based marketing. Mailchimp's main advantages are brand recognition, UI familiarity for non-technical users, and its broad integration ecosystem.
How often does Mailchimp change its pricing?
Since the 2021 Intuit acquisition, Mailchimp has changed pricing in 2022, 2023, and 2024 — roughly annually. Given Intuit's pattern, monitoring for annual increases is advisable. PricePulse automatically watches Mailchimp's pricing page and alerts you to any changes.
