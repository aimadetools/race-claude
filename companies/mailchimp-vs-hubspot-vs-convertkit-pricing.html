Which Email Platform Should You Choose?

Choose Mailchimp if...

You're a small business or local brand with a modest list (under 5K) that needs basic email marketing — newsletters, promotions, simple automations. Mailchimp's brand recognition, templates, and integrations make it the safest default for non-technical marketers. Watch out: at 10K+ contacts, consider switching to ConvertKit unless you need Mailchimp's advanced segmentation.

Choose HubSpot if...

You need email marketing as part of a full inbound marketing stack — CRM, blog, SEO tools, lead scoring, and attribution reporting all in one platform. HubSpot's free and Starter tiers are excellent entry points. But be honest with yourself: if you only need email marketing, HubSpot Professional at $890/month is dramatically overpriced vs. alternatives. HubSpot wins when the whole suite is used.

Choose ConvertKit (Kit) if...

You're a creator — writer, podcaster, YouTuber, online educator — who wants to build and monetize an email audience. ConvertKit's paid newsletter tools, subscriber tagging, and simple automation are purpose-built for creator businesses. Its 10K-subscriber free tier is the most generous in the market. It's the obvious choice for anyone building an audience-based business rather than a traditional e-commerce or B2B funnel.

Mailchimp vs HubSpot vs ConvertKit FAQ

Which email marketing tool has the best free plan?
ConvertKit wins for list size — free up to 10,000 subscribers with landing pages and basic automations. HubSpot's free tier is limited to 2,000 sends/month. Mailchimp's free tier caps at 500 contacts. If you're just starting out and want to grow a newsletter, ConvertKit's free plan is the most permissive.
Why is HubSpot Marketing Hub so expensive at Professional tier?
HubSpot Professional ($890/month) is priced as a full marketing platform — blog hosting, SEO tools, landing pages, smart content, A/B testing, and automation — not just an email marketing tool. If you use all those features, the ROI can be there. If you only need email marketing and basic automation, you're paying for a lot of features you won't use. Mailchimp Standard or ConvertKit Creator provide 80% of the email-specific functionality for 95% less cost.
Did Mailchimp raise prices recently?
Yes. Mailchimp raised prices by 10–20% across plans in 2023, the first major price increase since Intuit acquired Mailchimp in 2021. The Essentials plan went from $9.99 to $13/month for 500 contacts. Mailchimp also tightened its free tier, reducing the free contact limit from 2,000 to 500. Many small businesses migrated to alternatives (ConvertKit, Brevo, Moosend) following this change.
