Who Should Choose Microsoft 365

Microsoft 365 is better if you:

  • Need desktop Office apps — Excel on desktop is irreplaceable for finance, accounting, and data analysis. There's no Google Sheets equivalent for complex models.
  • Are already on Windows with Azure AD / Intune — Switching carries significant IT migration cost. The Microsoft ecosystem benefits compound when you're already in it.
  • Work in regulated industries — Healthcare (HIPAA), government (FedRAMP), legal — M365 E3/E5 compliance features are best-in-class. Some frameworks require Microsoft specifically.
  • Don't need AI yet — If you're not adding Copilot/Gemini, M365 is cheaper at the Business Basic and Standard tiers.
  • Heavy PowerPoint users — Complex slide decks, animations, and presentations still render better in PowerPoint than Google Slides.

Who Should Choose Google Workspace

Google Workspace is better if you:

  • Are a new or early-stage company — Workspace is significantly easier to set up, manage, and scale. No Active Directory expertise needed. Onboarding a new employee takes minutes.
  • Work cross-platform (Mac, Linux, Windows, Chromebook) — Everything runs in the browser. No "works better on Windows" friction.
  • Want AI at lower cost — Gemini Business at $10/user vs Copilot at $30/user. Same AI capabilities, 3× the price difference.
  • Need strong real-time collaboration — Google Docs' simultaneous editing is still better than M365's co-authoring in practice. Fewer conflicts, faster sync.
  • Remote-first company — Google Meet + Docs + Drive is purpose-built for distributed teams. Less infrastructure required.
  • Startups or cost-sensitive teams — Workspace Business Starter for a 10-person team costs $864/year. Add Gemini: $2,064/year. M365 equivalent with Copilot: $5,100/year.

Migration Cost Reality Check

Don't switch just to save $100/month. The true cost of switching includes:

  • IT migration time: Email migration (2-4 weeks for 20 users), file migration, settings reconfiguration
  • Staff retraining: 1-3 hours per employee for basic retraining, longer for power Excel users
  • Process breaks: Excel macros, SharePoint workflows, Outlook rules — all need rebuilding in new platform
  • Temporary productivity loss: 2-4 weeks of reduced output during transition

Rule of thumb: For a 20-person company, migration typically costs $5,000-$20,000 in staff time and consultant fees. Only switch if you'll save more than that over 2-3 years — or if you're building a new company from scratch.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Microsoft 365 or Google Workspace better value in 2026?
For most companies not adding AI: Microsoft 365 is slightly cheaper at the Business Standard tier ($12.50 vs $14.40). For companies adding AI assistants: Google Workspace is significantly cheaper — Gemini Business ($10/user) vs Copilot ($30/user). If your team is cloud-first and doesn't need desktop Office, Workspace wins on price. If you need Excel or are already on Windows, M365 likely wins on total cost.
Can you run both Microsoft 365 and Google Workspace simultaneously?
Yes, many companies do. A common pattern: Google Workspace for email, calendar, and collaboration; Microsoft 365 Apps (desktop Office only, no email) for Excel and Word. This costs ~$15-20/user/month total and gets the best of both. Not efficient for large companies but works for smaller teams with mixed needs.
Which is better for a 5-person startup?
Google Workspace Business Starter — $36/month total. Simpler to set up, no IT expertise needed, great for collaboration. If you need Excel for financial models, add M365 Apps for Business ($8.25/user) — $41/user/month total for both, still cheaper than M365 Business Standard with Copilot.
Which has better security and compliance?
For most SMBs, both are adequate. For regulated industries (healthcare, finance, government), Microsoft 365 E3/E5 is more established — particularly for FedRAMP, HIPAA, and eDiscovery requirements. Some government contracts specifically mandate Microsoft. Google Workspace Enterprise also has strong compliance, but M365 is the default choice in regulated sectors.
Will either platform raise prices again in 2026?
