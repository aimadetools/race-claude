Microsoft 365 Copilot Add-On

$30/user/month — Microsoft 365 Copilot is an AI assistant that works across Teams, Word, Excel, and Outlook. It generates meeting summaries, drafts emails, analyzes documents, and runs in Teams chat. It requires M365 Business Standard or higher to add.

This is the steepest AI add-on in the market. A team of 10 on Business Standard + Copilot pays $12.50 + $30 = $42.50/user/month — more than most standalone tools.

Microsoft Teams / M365 Price History

Microsoft has increased M365 pricing twice in three years, framing each increase around new AI and security capabilities.

M365 Business Plans Price Increase — +10–15% March 2025
Microsoft raised M365 Business Basic from $6 to $6 (held), Business Standard from $12.50 to $12.50 (held in US), and Business Premium from $20 to $22/user/month. The increases were larger in some regions. Copilot was cited as the driver for premium tier increases.
Business Premium: $20/user/mo→$22/user/mo
Teams Separated from M365 in EU October 2023
Under EU regulatory pressure (antitrust investigation), Microsoft unbundled Teams from M365 in the European Economic Area. European customers could buy M365 without Teams at a slight discount, or buy Teams separately. This change affected pricing structures and forced Microsoft to price Teams explicitly.
M365 Price Increase — First in a Decade March 2022
Microsoft raised M365 Commercial prices by $1–2/user/month, the first price increase in 10 years. Business Basic went from $5 to $6/user/month (+20%). Business Standard went from $12.50 (held). Microsoft framed this around security investments and Teams growth.
Business Basic: $5/user/mo→$6/user/mo
Teams Launched as Slack Competitor 2017–2020
Teams launched in 2017 bundled free with Office 365 and grew explosively during COVID-19 (2020). By 2022, Teams had 270M+ monthly active users, largely due to M365 bundling. This effectively made Teams the default choice for any organization already paying for M365.

Is Microsoft Teams Worth It vs. Zoom and Slack?

The bundle math is what makes Teams uniquely compelling — and uniquely complicated.

If you're already paying for M365: Teams is free. This is true for the vast majority of enterprise customers. If your organization uses Outlook email and Office apps, you're almost certainly on M365, and Teams costs $0 additional. In this case, Teams vs. Slack is really "free vs. $7.25/user/month." Teams wins purely on cost.

If you're not on M365: You're comparing Teams Essentials ($4/user/month, no email/Office) against Zoom Pro ($15.99/user/month), Google Meet (free with Workspace), or Slack ($7.25/user/month). Teams Essentials is the cheapest paid option for pure video/chat collaboration.

The Copilot trap: Adding Microsoft 365 Copilot for AI features costs $30/user/month on top of your M365 plan. A 50-person team on Business Standard + Copilot spends $2,125/month. Compare that to Zoom's AI features included in Business ($21.99/seat) at $1,099/month. Copilot is powerful but expensive.

Microsoft Teams Pricing FAQ

Is Microsoft Teams free?
Yes — Teams has a free plan with unlimited chat, file sharing, and 60-minute group video meetings for up to 100 people. It's also included at no extra charge in all paid Microsoft 365 Business and Enterprise plans. For most businesses already paying for M365, Teams costs nothing additional.
What's the difference between Teams Essentials and M365 Business Basic?
Teams Essentials ($4/user/month) gives you just Teams — meetings, chat, collaboration — with no email or Office apps. M365 Business Basic ($6/user/month) includes Teams plus Exchange email (50 GB mailbox), SharePoint, 1 TB OneDrive, and web/mobile Office apps. For $2 more, Basic is almost always the better value.
Does Microsoft Teams have meeting recording?
Meeting recordings are available on paid plans (Teams Essentials and above). On M365 plans, recordings are saved to OneDrive or SharePoint. The free Teams plan does not include meeting recording. Recording retention policies and storage limits vary by plan.
Is Microsoft 365 Copilot worth $30/user/month?
Copilot provides AI-generated meeting summaries, action items, email drafting, and document analysis across Teams, Outlook, and Office apps. For knowledge workers spending 6+ hours/week in meetings, the time savings can justify the cost. For most SMBs, it's expensive. Microsoft typically runs quarterly pilots — consider a limited seat trial before buying org-wide.
How often does Microsoft raise M365/Teams prices?
Microsoft raised M365 prices for the first time in a decade in March 2022, and again (for some plans) in early 2025. With Copilot AI investments and infrastructure cost pressure, further increases are possible. Annual contract customers are protected for their contract term; month-to-month customers should watch for notices. PricePulse monitors Microsoft's pricing pages automatically.
