Which Should You Choose?

Choose Notion if: You need an all-in-one workspace for docs, databases, CRM, and wiki. Your team wants flexibility and customization. You value cost per seat (Notion is cheaper for larger teams).

Choose Asana if: You're focused on project management and need advanced timelines, portfolios, and resource planning. You want a specialized tool built for team project work. You need time tracking built-in.

Use both if: Many teams use Notion for documentation and Asana for project management. They complement each other — Notion for knowledge, Asana for execution.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Notion or Asana cheaper?

Notion is significantly cheaper. A 10-person team costs $180/year on Notion Business vs $1,318/year on Asana Starter. However, they solve different problems — Notion is all-in-one, Asana is specialized.

Can I use Notion for project management?

Yes, using Notion's database feature you can build project management systems, kanban boards, and task tracking. However, Asana's project management is more purpose-built with timelines, resource planning, and dependencies.

Can I use Asana for documentation?

Asana has limited documentation features. Notion's wiki and document tools are far superior. If you need a strong knowledge base, Notion wins.

Did Asana raise prices in 2026?

Yes. In January 2026, Asana increased all plan prices. Starter went from $9 to $10.99, Standard from $15 to $18, and Advanced from $20 to $24.99.

Should we use both?

Many teams do. Notion for documentation and wikis, Asana for project management. The two tools are complementary, not competitive. Combined cost for 10 people: ~$525/month (Asana) if you keep Notion free.
