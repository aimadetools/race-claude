Pricing Model Differences

Notion: Per-User Pricing

Each team member needs their own Pro ($20) or Team ($25) plan. Cost scales linearly with team size.

5 people $100/mo (Pro)
25 people $625/mo (Team)

Coda: Workspace-Based Pricing

One Team plan ($20/mo) covers up to 20 team members. Flat rate regardless of team size (under 20).

5 people $20/mo (Team)
25 people $30/mo (Pro)

Evernote: Per-User Pricing (Teams)

Each user on Teams plan costs $14.99/mo. Also per-user for individual Premium ($9.99).

5 people $74.95/mo (Teams)
25 people $374.75/mo (Teams)

When to Choose Each

🏆 Choose Notion if:
  • You need a true all-in-one platform (docs + databases + wikis + projects)
  • Your team is small (1–3 people), making per-user cost acceptable
  • You want the largest ecosystem of integrations and templates
  • You plan to build internal databases and automations
🏆 Choose Coda if:
  • You have a team of 5+ people (best ROI with flat $20/mo for up to 20 users)
  • You need collaborative document editing with automation
  • You want spreadsheet-like functionality with better UI than Notion tables
  • Your primary use is documentation and team coordination
🏆 Choose Evernote if:
  • You prioritize note-taking over collaboration (no databases or tables)
  • You need offline access to all notes
  • You want the best web clipper (save web content to notes)
  • You're an individual or very small team (solo cost is cheap)

Key Insights

  • Coda dominates for teams 5–20 people: The flat $20/mo Team plan covers everyone. Notion and Evernote scale linearly, making them expensive for mid-sized teams.
  • Notion for ambitious all-in-one needs: If you need databases, formulas, and wikis all in one place, Notion is the only true choice (Coda is docs-first).
  • Evernote for pure note-taking: Cheapest for individuals ($9.99/mo), but loses on features and team collaboration. Best if you only need notes, not projects/docs.
  • Notion raised prices in March 2026: Plus ($8→$10) and Team ($15→$18). Coda and Evernote prices stable.
