Which Should You Choose?

Choose Notion if: You need an all-in-one workspace for docs, databases, CRM, and wiki. You're already invested in the Notion ecosystem. Your team values flexibility over specialized tooling.

Choose Linear if: You're a development or product team focused on issue tracking. You want a focused, fast tool instead of an all-in-one. You need rich APIs and webhooks for integration.

Use both if: You use Notion for documentation and Linear for issue tracking. Many teams do exactly this — they're complementary, not competitors.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Notion or Linear cheaper?

For most teams, Linear is cheaper because it charges per team, not per member. A 10-person team pays $7/month ($84/year) for Linear Starter vs $180/year for Notion Business. However, if you only need 1-2 paid members, Notion may be competitive.

Can I use Notion and Linear together?

Yes, many teams do. Notion works well for documentation, wikis, and databases. Linear is specialized for issue tracking. Using both often costs less and provides better specialization than using either alone.

Does Notion have project management features?

Yes, through its database feature. You can create kanban boards, timelines, and task management in Notion. However, Linear is purpose-built for this and has more advanced features like automations and integrations.

Does Linear have a wiki or documentation feature?

Linear has basic wiki features, but Notion's documentation tools are much more powerful. If you need strong docs, Notion (or a dedicated wiki tool) is better.

What's Linear's free plan limit?

Linear's free plan supports up to 10 team members. Once you exceed 10, you must upgrade to Starter ($7/month). Notion's free plan has more limited features (10 guests, limited history) but unlimited personal use.
