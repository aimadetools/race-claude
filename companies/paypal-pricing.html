PayPal vs Stripe Comparison

Stripe (2.9% + $0.30) is typically $0.60 cheaper per $100 transaction than PayPal. However, PayPal's strength is customer trust — 400M+ users recognize the PayPal brand, increasing conversion rates by 5–10% for merchants with a mix of buyer types.

Strategic consideration: Many e-commerce businesses offer both payment options (Stripe + PayPal) to capture maximum conversion. Stripe handles card checkout; PayPal handles PayPal-preference buyers. For B2B SaaS, Stripe's superior subscription billing features make it the primary choice.

When to Choose PayPal

  • Large PayPal user base: If 20%+ of your customers pay with PayPal balance, the brand recognition is worth the slight fee premium
  • E-commerce + B2C: Conversion lift from PayPal option typically outweighs 0.5–0.6% fee difference
  • International payments: PayPal has strong presence in emerging markets (India, Southeast Asia)
  • Physical + Online mix: PayPal Here and in-person readers integrate with online payments

When to Choose Stripe Instead

  • SaaS/Subscriptions: Stripe's subscription management is far superior to PayPal
  • Custom checkout: Stripe's developer-friendly APIs allow full UX customization
  • Cost-sensitive: 0.6% savings compound significantly at $1M+ annual volume
  • Usage-based billing: Stripe's metered billing handles this; PayPal cannot

Frequently Asked Questions

What is PayPal's standard processing fee?

PayPal's standard rate is 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction for customers paying with a PayPal account. For non-account holders (guest checkout), the fee increases to 3.49% + $0.30. Both rates are lower than credit card processing at traditional banks.

Does PayPal charge monthly fees?

PayPal offers a free Starter tier with no monthly fees. Premium subscription tiers ($10–$30/mo) add advanced reporting, API access, and priority support. Most small businesses never upgrade from the free tier.

How much more expensive is PayPal than Stripe?

For standard transactions: Stripe is 0.6% + $0.30 cheaper per transaction. On a $100 payment, Stripe costs $3.20 vs PayPal's $3.80. However, this assumes all PayPal payments are guest checkout (3.49% rate). For PayPal account holders (2.9%), the cost difference shrinks to $0.00, and the decision shifts to features and customer preference.

Can I negotiate PayPal's rates?

PayPal rarely negotiates transaction fees for small merchants. However, high-volume sellers ($1M+/year) can contact PayPal Sales to discuss volume discounts. Large sellers typically cannot achieve the same discount as Stripe offers.

What are PayPal's international payment fees?

PayPal charges 2.9% + $0.30 + an additional 2–4% cross-border fee depending on the destination country. For example: UK transfers add ~3.5%, Canada adds ~2.5%, India adds ~4%. This makes PayPal more expensive than Stripe for cross-border SaaS payments.
