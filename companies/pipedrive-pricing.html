Pipedrive Pricing History (2024–2026)

Pipedrive has kept pricing relatively stable compared to competitors, but restructured its tier system in 2025-2026 to simplify entry-level options and upsell Advanced tier features.

Pricing tier restructure January 2026
Pipedrive introduced the "Essential" tier at $14/user/month to compete with free/freemium CRM tools. The old "Starter" tier ($12/user/mo) was renamed and aligned with this new entry-level position. The Advanced tier was repositioned from $39 to $44/user/month, bundling in basic automation. Professional tier (formerly $79) moved to $89/user/month with enhanced forecasting tools.
Starter → Essential:$12/user/mo→$14/user/mo
Advanced:$39/user/mo→$44/user/mo (+13%)
Professional:$79/user/mo→$89/user/mo (+13%)
Advanced tier gets workflow automation August 2025
Pipedrive added workflow automation to the Advanced tier (previously Professional-only). This move was designed to reduce churn by making Advanced more valuable while positioning Professional as the premium option for enterprises.
Advanced features:Basic pipelines + forecasting→+ Workflow automation
Free trial reduced from 14 to 7 days May 2025
Pipedrive shortened its free trial period from 14 days to 7 days in May 2025. This change increased conversion pressure and reduced time for users to fully evaluate the platform. Existing free-tier users were grandfathered through June 2025.
Free trial:14 days→7 days
Annual billing discount increased February 2025
Pipedrive increased its annual billing discount from 15% to 20%, effectively incentivizing longer-term commitments. This move retained high-risk monthly subscribers while generating predictable revenue.
Annual discount:15%→20%

What Pipedrive's Pricing Changes Mean for Sales Teams

A 5-person sales team on Advanced tier pays $220/month ($2,640/year) as of April 2026. Had they been grandfathered at the old $39 rate, they'd pay $195/month instead — a difference of $300/year. With the 20% annual discount ($176/month), the deal becomes more attractive for committed customers.

Pipedrive's strategy is working: by introducing Essential at $14/user/month, they're capturing founders and solopreneurs, then upselling them to Advanced ($44) as they grow. The 13% price increase on Advanced/Professional is modest compared to Salesforce or Asana, making Pipedrive attractive as a cost-conscious alternative.

If you're comparing sales CRMs, Pipedrive's total cost of ownership is one of the lowest, especially with annual prepayment. HubSpot is free for basic CRM, making it appealing for small teams, but Pipedrive wins for sales-focused workflows and pipeline visualization.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does Pipedrive cost for a small sales team?

For a 5-person sales team on Advanced (as of April 2026), the cost is $220/month on monthly billing. With annual prepayment (20% discount), that drops to $176/month or $2,112/year. Professional tier costs $445/month on monthly billing, $356/month with annual discount.

Is there a free version of Pipedrive?

Pipedrive offers a 7-day free trial for all plans (recently reduced from 14 days). There is no permanently free plan. Essential tier at $14/user/month is the cheapest paid option, making it accessible for solopreneurs.

How does Pipedrive compare to HubSpot and Salesforce?

HubSpot CRM is free for up to 3 users. Pipedrive Essential is $14/user/month ($70/month for 5 users). Salesforce Essentials is $180/user/month ($900 for 5 users). For small sales teams, HubSpot is cheapest; for mid-market teams, Pipedrive offers the best value; for enterprise with complex needs, Salesforce is standard but expensive.

Does Pipedrive offer discounts for longer contracts?

Yes, Pipedrive offers a 20% discount for annual prepayment. Multi-year contracts (3 years) may qualify for additional discounts — contact Pipedrive sales for a quote. Enterprise customers almost always negotiate discounts above 20%.

Will Pipedrive continue to raise prices?

Based on Pipedrive's pattern (restructure in 2026), the next significant change is likely in Q1-Q2 2027. Unlike Salesforce or Asana, Pipedrive has been relatively conservative with pricing.
