Shopify charges $39–$399/mo plus transaction fees. WooCommerce is free but needs hosting and plugins. Etsy charges 9.7% combined fees on every sale — which eats $9,700/year on $100K in revenue. Here's the full cost picture.

When to Choose Each

Choose Shopify if...

You want a fully managed e-commerce store with no technical overhead. Best for brands that need a professional storefront, dropshipping, POS, or serious scale. At $39/mo + Shopify Payments, total costs are reasonable and predictable. Switch to Advanced ($399/mo) once you're doing $50K+/mo in sales to cut transaction fees.

Choose WooCommerce if...

You're already on WordPress, have a developer on staff, or want maximum control and zero platform fees. WooCommerce is the cheapest option at scale since you only pay payment processing fees. The trade-off: you maintain hosting, security, updates, and plugins yourself (or pay someone to).

Choose Etsy if...

You sell handmade, vintage, or craft goods and want to tap Etsy's 95M built-in buyers without building your own audience. Perfect for side hustles and small sellers under $2K/mo in sales. At higher revenue, Etsy's 9.7% combined fees become brutal — most successful Etsy sellers add a Shopify store once they're profitable.

Bottom Line

By cost at scale: WooCommerce < Shopify << Etsy. Etsy becomes the most expensive option above $2,000/mo in sales due to combined fees approaching 10%.

By ease of use: Etsy > Shopify > WooCommerce. Etsy is the easiest to start selling on — no store to build. Shopify is plug-and-play. WooCommerce requires technical comfort.

Best strategy: Start on Etsy to validate demand → move to Shopify as revenue grows → consider WooCommerce if you have WordPress expertise and want to eliminate platform fees entirely.
