What Slack's Price Increases Mean for the Market

Two consecutive price increases signal Salesforce's monetization pressure on Slack. For competitors like Microsoft Teams (included with M365 subscriptions), Discord for Work, and emerging tools like Campfire, this is a significant opportunity window.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Slack still have a free plan in 2026?

Yes. The free plan limits message history to 90 days and allows only 10 app integrations. For teams that need full history or tools like Google Drive, GitHub, or Jira connected, Pro ($10/seat/month annual) is the minimum viable plan.

How does Slack compare to Microsoft Teams pricing?

Microsoft Teams is included with Microsoft 365 Business plans starting at $6/user/month. For organizations already using M365, Teams has no marginal cost. This is Slack's biggest competitive threat, which may be driving Slack to justify its price premium with AI features (Slack AI launched 2024).

Is Slack's annual discount worth it in 2026?

The annual discount was reduced from 20% to 15% in January 2026. At the current Pro rate of $10/month billed monthly or ~$8.50/month billed annually, the 15% discount still saves a meaningful amount for larger teams but is less compelling than before.

Can I lock in current pricing before the next increase?

Annual plans lock in the current price for 12 months. If you expect Slack to raise prices again (which, based on recent history, is likely), signing an annual plan before the next increase announcement is a common strategy. Get PricePulse alerts to know when an increase is coming.
