When to Choose Each

Choose Slack If:

  • You run a business or startup — Slack is built for professional teams
  • You need message history retention — Slack pro guarantee that nothing gets lost (90 days free, full history paid)
  • You need integrations — 2,000+ apps (Salesforce, Jira, GitHub, Asana, etc.) connect directly
  • You need compliance — Audit logs and SSO for regulated industries
  • Your team values professionalism — Slack's tone is business-focused

Choose Discord If:

  • You're building a community — Discord is optimized for large groups (thousands of people)
  • You can't afford messaging costs — Discord free is genuinely free forever
  • You need voice/video by default — Discord's voice channels are first-class (not add-on)
  • You're managing gaming, creative, or open-source communities — Discord is the standard
  • You want flexibility with roles and permissions — Discord's permission system is powerful and free

Hybrid Approach

  • Use Slack for internal team communication — Your employees, contractors, partners
  • Use Discord for community — Your customers, users, fans, open-source contributors
  • Total cost: Small team ($495/yr Slack) + Free Discord community = $495/yr
  • Benefit: Best tool for each audience without compromise

Frequently Asked Questions

Can Discord replace Slack for business?

Partially, but not ideally. Discord's free tier works, but lacks audit logs, SSO, and message search is clunky. Discord is great for async chat with communities but lacks Slack's workflow automation and native integrations. For compliance-heavy industries, Discord doesn't cut it.

Can Slack replace Discord for communities?

No. Slack's per-user cost makes it prohibitively expensive for large communities (100+ members). Discord's permission system, voice channels, and server boost model are designed for communities. Slack is 1:1 or team messaging.

What about Teams vs Slack?

Teams is cheaper if you're already using Office 365. But for standalone messaging, Slack has better integrations and UX. Teams shines in enterprises that need Office suite integration.

Is Discord secure for business?

Discord is secure for casual internal teams. But lack of audit logs, SSO, and compliance certifications make it unsuitable for healthcare, finance, or government. Use Slack for sensitive data.

Can I run both Slack and Discord?

Yes, and many companies do. Slack for internal teams ($495/yr for 5 people), Discord free for community or customer support. Total cost: ~$495/yr.

Which has better customer support?

Slack has phone support (Business+ tier). Discord support is community-driven and slower. For enterprise issues, Slack wins.
