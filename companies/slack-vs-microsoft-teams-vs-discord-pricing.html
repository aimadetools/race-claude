When to Choose Each

Choose Slack if...

You're a tech startup or product team that lives in integrations. Slack's 2,600+ app marketplace, Workflow Builder, and Huddles are unmatched. Worth $7.25/user/mo if you're building with tools like GitHub, Jira, PagerDuty, or Salesforce. Loses value if your team mostly just sends messages.

Choose Microsoft Teams if...

You're an enterprise or mid-market company already on Microsoft 365. You're almost certainly paying $6–$12.50/user/mo for M365 already — Teams is included at zero marginal cost. Deep integration with Outlook, SharePoint, and Teams Rooms makes it the obvious default for Office-centric organizations.

Choose Discord if...

You're building a developer community, open-source project, or creator community — not an enterprise team. Discord is free, has voice channels, and is the default platform for gaming/dev communities. Not suitable for sensitive business data or compliance-regulated industries (no SSO, no data export).

The Key Differences

Slack vs Teams: These are both business communication tools, but Slack has better third-party integrations and a cleaner UX for most teams. Teams wins on price (if you're already on M365) and has better video/meeting features through integration with the full Microsoft stack.

Slack vs Discord: Completely different use cases. Slack is for internal business teams, Discord is for communities (external, public, or semi-public). Discord shouldn't be in your shortlist if you're evaluating business communication tools — it lacks enterprise security features entirely.

Teams vs Discord: No real competition. Teams is enterprise-grade, Discord is community-grade. Some developer-focused teams use both: Teams for internal business, Discord for their external developer community.
