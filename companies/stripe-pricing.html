Stripe Pricing History (2024–2026)

Stripe has historically kept its headline rate stable, but has consistently expanded the list of add-on fees. Here's every verified change in the past two years:

International card surcharge introduced January 2026
Stripe unbundled international card processing costs for US-based merchants. Previously, the 2.9% rate absorbed international card overhead. The new surcharge applies to any card issued outside the US, including most European and Asian cards.
International card rate:2.9% + $0.30 (all cards)→2.9% + $0.30 + 0.4% surcharge
Instant payout fee increase January 2026
Stripe raised the instant payout fee quietly via a pricing page update. Accounts using instant payouts for cash flow management saw their effective cost increase by ~17%. The change was not communicated by email to affected accounts.
Instant payouts:1.5%→1.75%
Stripe Tax billing model change Q3 2025
Stripe Tax moved from a flat monthly fee model to a per-transaction percentage model. High-volume merchants with lower average order values saw significant cost increases. Low-volume merchants with high AOV benefited.
Stripe Tax:$0.02/transaction + flat fee→0.5% of taxable transactions
Chargeback protection program expanded Q2 2024
Stripe expanded its chargeback protection program from invite-only to available for all eligible accounts. The opt-in fee (0.4% per transaction) covers the cost of chargebacks including the dispute fee, which otherwise costs $15 per chargeback.
Chargeback protection:Invite-only→Available to all (0.4%)

What Stripe's Fee Changes Mean for SaaS Companies

For a SaaS company processing $50,000/month with 30% international customers, the new international card surcharge adds approximately $600/month in fees — $7,200/year — with no corresponding product improvement.

The instant payout change particularly affects bootstrapped companies and marketplace operators who rely on fast cash access. The 0.25% increase may seem small, but at scale it compounds: a company doing $500K/month in instant payouts pays an extra $1,250/month.

If you sell a competing payment processor (Paddle, Lemon Squeezy, Braintree, Adyen), these Stripe fee increases are an acquisition opportunity — but only if you know about them before your competitors run campaigns.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Stripe's standard processing fee in 2026?

Stripe's standard rate remains 2.9% + $0.30 per successful card charge for US merchants processing US-issued cards. International cards now carry an additional 0.4% surcharge as of January 2026, bringing the effective rate for international transactions to 3.3% + $0.30.

Does Stripe charge a monthly fee?

Stripe's core processing has no monthly fee — you only pay per transaction. However, add-on products like Stripe Radar For Fraud Teams ($0.05/screened charge), Stripe Tax (0.5%), and Stripe Sigma (custom analytics, $0.02/charge up to a cap) all carry additional costs that can add up for growing businesses.

How does Stripe compare to Paddle for SaaS?

Paddle acts as a Merchant of Record (handles VAT/sales tax compliance automatically) while Stripe requires you to configure Stripe Tax separately. Paddle's all-in rate is typically 5% + $0.50, which appears higher than Stripe's headline rate but includes tax compliance that would otherwise cost 0.5%+ via Stripe Tax. The right choice depends on your international revenue mix and how much you value compliance automation.

Will Stripe negotiate lower rates?

Yes, but typically only at $1M+ in annual processing volume. Custom rates usually reduce the percentage component (e.g., 2.5% instead of 2.9%) while keeping the flat fee. Contact Stripe's sales team — they have discretion on volume pricing.

How do I monitor Stripe for pricing changes?

Stripe doesn't send advance email notice for all fee changes — the January 2026 international card surcharge was only communicated via a pricing page update. The most reliable way is to set up an automated monitor that alerts you within hours of any change to Stripe's pricing page.
