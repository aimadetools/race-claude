Which Should You Choose?

Choose Stripe if: You're building a SaaS or marketplace. You need modern APIs and webhooks. You want developer flexibility. You prefer no monthly minimums. You want simplicity (one-tier pricing).

Choose PayPal if: You're selling physical goods (higher buyer familiarity). You need global payment methods. You want established brand trust. You're already integrated with eBay or PayPal ecosystem. You prefer flat-rate alternatives (Plus tier at high volume).

Key insight: For most businesses, Stripe and PayPal have identical transaction fees (2.2% + $0.30). The difference is whether you pay a monthly fee. Stripe wins for developers and SaaS; PayPal wins for ecommerce and buyer familiarity. Your choice should be based on features (API quality, subscriptions, integrations) rather than fees.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Stripe or PayPal cheaper?

For standard transactions under $50, they're effectively the same. Both charge 2.2% + $0.30. PayPal's Plus tier ($30/mo) can save money at very high volume (13,000+ transactions/month), but Stripe has no monthly fees. For most businesses, cost is a tie — choose based on features instead.

Did Stripe raise prices in 2026?

Yes. In January 2026, Stripe increased international transaction fees from 2.2% to 3.2%, a 10-percentage-point increase. This only affects non-US card transactions. Domestic US card rates (2.2%) remain unchanged.

Does PayPal have hidden fees?

PayPal's fee structure is simpler than it used to be, but the company still charges for extras like faster payouts, currency conversion, and chargebacks. Base transaction rates are transparent. Stripe generally has fewer surprise fees.

Which is better for subscriptions?

Stripe has native subscription support and is purpose-built for SaaS. PayPal supports subscriptions but it's less elegant. For recurring billing, Stripe is the clear winner.

Which has better developer experience?

Stripe. The API is modern, documentation is excellent, and webhooks are reliable. PayPal's API is older and less developer-friendly. If you're building custom integrations, Stripe is significantly easier.
