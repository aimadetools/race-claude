Typeform Pricing History (2024–2026)

Typeform has progressively restricted its free plan while raising paid tier prices. Here's the complete record:

Free plan response limit slashed from 100 to 10/month February 2026
Typeform reduced free plan responses from 100/month to 10/month — a 90% reduction. Indie founders using Typeform for lead capture forms, product feedback, or onboarding surveys found their tools effectively broken overnight. Teams exceeding 10 responses/month had to upgrade or switch tools immediately.
Free responses/month:100→10
Basic plan price increase Q4 2025
Typeform raised Basic plan pricing from $15/month to $25/month — a 67% increase. The number of included responses stayed the same (100/month). Teams that previously used Basic as a cost-effective entry point were effectively pushed toward Plus or alternatives.
Basic (monthly):$15/mo→$25/mo
Free plan: 3 forms limit removed, response limit added Q2 2025
Typeform changed the free plan structure: removed the 3-form limit (all plans now include unlimited forms) while simultaneously introducing a 100 responses/month cap. Previously, free users could have 3 forms with unlimited responses. The net effect was worse for most active users.
Free forms:3 forms, unlimited responses→Unlimited forms, 100 responses/mo
Typeform AI (conversational AI forms) launched Q1 2025
Typeform launched AI-powered form generation and conversational AI follow-up questions. Available on Plus and above. Used to justify subsequent price increases across all tiers.
AI forms:Not available→Plus+ plans

What Typeform's Restrictions Mean for Competitors

A 90% cut in free plan responses is one of the most aggressive free-tier restrictions any major SaaS company made in 2026. At 10 responses/month, the free plan is essentially a demo-only tier — it's not usable for real business operations.

The immediate beneficiaries were Tally (genuinely free, unlimited responses), Google Forms (free, unlimited), Jotform (50 responses free), and Fillout (unlimited responses on free). All four saw significant signup spikes in the days after Typeform's February 2026 change.

If you sell any form builder or survey tool, Typeform's ongoing free tier erosion is a continuous acquisition opportunity. The key is detecting each change quickly — companies that responded within 48 hours captured the most displaced users.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many responses does Typeform free give you in 2026?

Typeform's free plan allows 10 responses per month as of February 2026 — down from 100/month before the change. This is effectively a demo-only tier. Any business use case (lead capture, feedback collection, surveys) will exceed this quickly and require a paid plan.

What's the cheapest way to collect unlimited form responses?

If you need more than 10 responses/month without paying, Typeform is no longer viable. Free alternatives with more generous limits include: Tally (unlimited responses, free), Google Forms (unlimited, free, no logic branching), Fillout (unlimited responses free with branding). If you need Typeform-quality design with conditional logic, Basic at $21/month (annual) is the entry point.

Is Typeform Basic worth $25/month?

At $25/month for 100 responses, Typeform Basic costs $0.25 per response — which is expensive compared to alternatives. It makes sense if you need Typeform's specific design quality (high-converting conversational UI) and only need light volume. For higher volumes, Plus ($42/month annual, 1,000 responses) is a better value at $0.042 per response.

Does Typeform offer a nonprofit discount?

Yes, Typeform offers 30% discounts for registered nonprofits and educational institutions. Apply through Typeform's website with proof of status.

What's the best Typeform alternative in 2026?

Depends on your needs: Tally for unlimited free responses with clean design; Jotform for enterprise features at lower cost; Google Forms for simplicity and free unlimited; Fillout for advanced logic and integrations on the free plan. If your priority is high-converting conversational forms and you're willing to pay, Typeform still leads on aesthetics — but the price-to-value ratio has deteriorated significantly since 2024.
