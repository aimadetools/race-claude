Which Should You Choose?

Choose Squarespace if...

You're a small business owner, photographer, restaurateur, or creative professional who wants a beautiful website without learning web design. Squarespace's templates are award-winning, the editor is intuitive, and the price is genuinely cheaper for solo use. If you're not a developer and don't need custom code, Squarespace is the better choice.

Choose Webflow if...

You're a designer or developer who needs full design control and resists template constraints, a freelancer or agency building custom sites for clients, or you need advanced CMS structures, custom interactions, or code export. Webflow's learning curve is real — expect 20–40 hours to become proficient. But once you are, you can build websites that are impossible in Squarespace.

Webflow vs Squarespace FAQ

Is Webflow or Squarespace cheaper?
Squarespace is cheaper for solo users and small businesses. Personal plan is $16/month (vs. Webflow Basic at $14/month), but Webflow requires a Workspace Plan for any team collaboration, which starts at $19/seat/month. A 2-person team on Webflow pays $38/month workspace + site plan fees. On Squarespace Business ($23/month), unlimited contributors are included. Squarespace wins on total cost for teams under 10.
Can I switch from Squarespace to Webflow?
You can, but it's not trivial. Webflow has import tools for basic content, but complex designs and CMS content require rebuilding. Most teams that switch find the migration takes 20–80 hours of design work to recreate the site properly in Webflow. The SEO impact is manageable with proper 301 redirects. Many teams use a redesign as the switching trigger rather than migrating an existing site 1:1.
Does Squarespace have a free plan?
Squarespace offers a free trial (14 days) but no permanent free tier. After the trial, you need a paid plan to keep the site live. Webflow has a free tier that lets you build and publish to a Webflow subdomain indefinitely — useful for prototyping and learning, but requires a paid Site Plan for a custom domain.
