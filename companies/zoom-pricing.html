Zoom Price Increase History

Zoom's pricing journey is a story of post-pandemic monetization pressure. After explosive growth during COVID-19, Zoom has been under pressure from Microsoft Teams (bundled with M365) and Google Meet (included in Workspace).

Pro Price Increase — +7% March 2024
Zoom raised Pro from $14.99 to $15.99/month per user. The modest increase was framed around the addition of AI Companion features, though the most powerful AI tools remained add-ons. Existing subscribers received ~30 days notice.
$14.99/user/month→$15.99/user/month
AI Companion Restructured as Add-On October 2023
Zoom launched AI Companion (meeting summaries, chat compose, whiteboard generation) as a paid add-on for lower tiers. The $9.99/month add-on effectively raised the cost of Pro users who needed AI features from $14.99 to $24.98/month.
Plan Consolidation: "Zoom One" Launch April 2023
Zoom rebranded plans under "Zoom One" to bundle meetings, phone, chat, and whiteboard. This restructuring increased the effective cost for teams that needed all features by consolidating previously separate pricing into bundled plans at higher per-seat costs.
Pro Plan Launch — Stable Pricing 2020–2022
Zoom Pro was priced at $14.99/month during the peak pandemic period. Despite 10× growth in user base, Zoom kept pricing stable to avoid bad press. The monetization shift began in 2023 as growth normalized and pressure from Teams/Meet intensified.

Is Zoom Worth It vs. Google Meet / Microsoft Teams?

The honest answer: for most SMBs, probably not anymore. Here's why:

Microsoft Teams: Included free with Microsoft 365 Business Basic ($6/user/month). If you're already paying for M365 (most teams are), Teams is effectively free — making Zoom's $15.99/seat a hard sell unless you need Zoom's specific UX or ecosystem integrations.

Google Meet: Included with Google Workspace Starter ($6/user/month). Same argument as Teams — if you're in Google's ecosystem, Meet is bundled at no extra cost.

When Zoom still wins: External meetings with non-corporate users (better UX for non-IT crowds), large webinars (Zoom Webinars is best-in-class), hardware room systems, and regulated industries needing Zoom's compliance features.

Zoom Pricing FAQ

Can I use Zoom free for business meetings?
Yes, but with a 40-minute limit on group meetings (3+ participants). For 1-on-1 meetings, the free tier is unlimited in duration. Most businesses find the 40-minute limit impractical and upgrade to Pro for longer meetings.
Does Zoom charge per user or per account?
Zoom Pro and Business charge per user per month (per licensed host). However, a single host can run meetings for unlimited attendees — you only need licenses for people who schedule and host meetings, not attendees. This makes small team pricing more reasonable: a 3-person company may only need 3 licenses even if guests join regularly.
Is AI Companion included in Zoom Pro?
As of 2024, Zoom includes AI Companion (meeting summaries, chat compose) in Pro at no additional cost for most features. However, advanced AI features like extended AI meeting summaries, AI-generated smart recordings with chapters, and workspace intelligence may require add-ons or higher-tier plans. The feature set changes frequently — always verify on Zoom's official pricing page.
How often does Zoom change its pricing?
Zoom has made significant pricing or plan restructuring changes in 2023 and 2024. With ongoing pressure to monetize AI features and differentiate from bundled competitors (Teams, Meet), further pricing changes are likely.
