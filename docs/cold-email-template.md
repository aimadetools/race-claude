## Outreach Strategy

### List Building
- LinkedIn: Search "SaaS founder" OR "product manager" OR "VP Growth"
- Indie Hackers: Browse top products, find founders
- Product Hunt: Look at recent launches in dev tools, SaaS categories
- Twitter: Search hashtags #SaaS #IndieHackers #Bootstrap
- Hacker News: "Show HN" posts from founders

### Target Profile
- Solo founders or small teams (1-10 people)
- $1K-50K MRR (engaged enough to pay, not enterprise-level yet)
- Have 3-10 direct competitors
- In SaaS/B2B space (pricing matters more)

### Outreach Sequence
1. **Day 1**: Send initial email (choose template based on research)
2. **Day 4**: If no reply, send follow-up #1 (Template 5 approach, acknowledge silence, re-frame value)
3. **Day 8**: If no reply, send final touch (no hard sell, just "worth trying free tier" + link)
4. **Day 12**: Stop and move to next prospect

### Expected Response Rates
- Open rate: 20-30% (founder email, personalized subject)
- Reply rate: 3-5% (for cold email)
- Click-through to product: 5-10%
- Signup rate: 30-50% of visitors
- Paid conversion rate: 10-15% of free users

### Personalization Checklist
- [ ] Use first name (not "Hi there")
- [ ] Mention specific competitor they likely track
- [ ] Mention specific trigger (pricing change you noticed, or generic pain point)
- [ ] One ask only (view the product, not a call)
- [ ] Keep it short (3-5 sentences max)
- [ ] Sign with your real name

### Don't Do This
- Don't be salesy ("This is a limited-time offer!")
- Don't be generic ("Dear SaaS founder")
- Don't overcomplicate ("We provide AI-powered competitive intelligence")
- Don't ask for a meeting first ("Would you be open to a 15-min call?")
- Don't spam lists (send one at a time, not CC everyone)

## Follow-Up Strategy

If someone replies, even with "not right now":

1. Thank them for the reply
2. Ask clarifying question: "What's blocking it?" or "When would this matter?"
3. Offer quick trial: "Happy to give you 3 months free to test if it helps"
4. Leave it open: "No pressure — happy to help if it becomes relevant later"

High-intent replies can become paying customers or good feedback sources.
