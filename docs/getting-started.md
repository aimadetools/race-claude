# Getting Started with PricePulse

Welcome to PricePulse! This guide will get you from signup to your first competitive pricing alert in under 10 minutes.

## Step 1: Sign Up (2 minutes)

1. Go to [getpricepulse.com](https://getpricepulse.com)
2. Click "Get Started" or "See Live Demo"
3. Enter your email address
4. Check your inbox for confirmation email (check spam if needed)
5. Click the confirmation link
6. You're now logged in and ready to monitor

## Step 2: Choose Your Plan (1 minute)

After confirming your email, you'll see the plan selection screen.

**Free Plan** (2 competitors)
- Great for: Testing the product, monitoring 1-2 key competitors
- Checks: Daily (once per day)
- Cost: $0

**Starter Plan** ($19/month)
- Great for: Most SaaS founders
- Checks: Hourly (up to 24x per day)
- Monitors: Up to 10 competitors
- Best value for the price

**Pro Plan** ($49/month)
- Great for: Serious competitive intelligence operations
- Checks: Every 30 minutes (48x per day)
- Monitors: Unlimited
- Includes: Slack/webhook integrations (coming soon)

**We recommend Starter** — it's 10x cheaper than alternatives and gives you hourly monitoring.

## Step 3: Add Your First Competitor (3 minutes)

1. Go to your [Dashboard](https://getpricepulse.com/dashboard.html)
2. Click "Add Monitor"
3. Paste the URL of a competitor's pricing page
4. Examples:
   - If you sell project management software: `https://asana.com/pricing`
   - If you sell analytics: `https://mixpanel.com/pricing`
   - Or any SaaS competitor you want to track
5. Click "Save Monitor"
6. You'll see "Monitor Added Successfully" — congrats!

**Pro Tip:** Add 3-5 competitors at once. The more you monitor, the better your market intelligence.

## Step 4: Your First Alert (happens automatically)

You don't need to do anything else. Here's what happens behind the scenes:

1. **Now:** PricePulse checks your competitor's pricing page
2. **Hourly:** If you have Starter or Pro, we check every hour
3. **Within minutes:** If something changes, you get an email alert with:
   - What changed (the exact diff)
   - Before/after comparison
   - Confidence score (to filter out noise)
   - Link to update your own pricing

## Understanding Your Dashboard

Your dashboard shows:

- **Monitor Status**: Green = active, Red = paused (too many errors)
- **Last Checked**: When we last monitored the page
- **Next Check**: When we'll check again
- **Alerts Count**: How many pricing changes detected so far
- **Monitor URL**: The competitor page we're tracking

## Tips for Success

### 1. Monitor the Right Pages
- ✅ Pricing pages (what we're optimized for)
- ✅ Pricing tables or plans pages
- ✅ Plan comparison pages
- ❌ Blog pages (too much noise)
- ❌ Homepage only (might not have pricing)

**Pro Tip:** Use the direct pricing page URL, not the homepage.

### 2. Monitor Direct Competitors First
- Who charges similar prices to you?
- Who targets the same customers?
- Who positions similarly?

These are your best benchmarks.

### 3. Add Aspirational Competitors Too
- Larger players in your space (how they've grown)
- Successful pricing models to learn from
- Companies that raised prices successfully

### 4. Check Your Dashboard Weekly
- Review alerts from the past week
- See if pricing trends are moving in a direction
- Update your own pricing if needed

### 5. Use Alerts to Inform Your Decisions
Example workflow:
1. Get alert: "Competitor X raised their Starter plan by $50"
2. Check your dashboard: You're still cheaper (good)
3. Ask yourself: Should you raise too? Are they justified?
4. Decide: Raise, stay flat, or emphasize your value

## Common Questions

### "What if I get false alerts?"
We filter out 97% of noise (cookie banners, dates, review counts). But if you get an alert you don't care about, just ignore it. We're always improving the filter.

### "How fast are the alerts?"
- Free plan: Once per day (same day as the change)
- Starter: Within 1 hour typically
- Pro: Within 30 minutes typically

### "Can I monitor a URL that requires login?"
Not yet — we can only monitor public-facing pages. We're working on auth support.

### "What if a competitor removes their pricing page?"
We'll catch that as a major change and alert you. It's unusual, but when it happens, it's important.

### "Can I export the alerts?"
Not yet, but you can view them in your dashboard. We're building CSV export soon.

### "What happens if I upgrade later?"
Upgrade anytime. You'll immediately start getting hourly checks instead of daily.

## Pro Tips for Power Users

### Track Price Trends Over Time
Keep a spreadsheet of competitor prices you've caught. After 2-3 months, you'll see patterns:
- Does Competitor X raise prices every Q2?
- Do they usually announce or surprise people?
- How much do they typically increase by?

### Use Alerts to Inform Your Roadmap
When competitors add features (visible in pricing page copy), it's a signal about market direction. You might want to build similar features or differentiate.

### Test Your Pricing Changes
When you raise or lower your prices, keep an eye on competitors. Do they follow within weeks? Or do they ignore you? That tells you something about market dynamics.

### Monitor Aspirational Competitors
Sign up for their pricing alerts too. Watch how successful (bigger) companies handle pricing. You're learning from their playbook.
