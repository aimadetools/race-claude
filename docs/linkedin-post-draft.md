SaaS is getting expensive in 2026.

My team of 3 uses 20 common tools (GitHub, Slack, Notion, ClickUp, Airtable, Asana, Figma, Typeform, Zapier, Adobe, HubSpot, Intercom, etc.).

Last year: $380/mo
This year: $520+/mo

That's a **$1,680/year increase** that nobody announced.

Most founders don't notice these until the credit card bill comes.

**The kicker?** Some tools are removing features from cheaper plans at the same time. You're paying more AND getting less.

**The why:** Inflation, investor pressure, competition for enterprise deals (they'll charge SMBs more to subsidize enterprise discounts).

ClickUp (Business tier): +58% overnight
GitHub Copilot: +90% ($10→$19/mo)
Typeform: +67%
Zapier: 2–5× depending on your automation volume
Airtable: +100% on some tiers

All within 4-6 month window.

3/ The pattern:
- Inflation + enterprise pricing pressure
- Features being removed from cheaper tiers (so you "need" to upgrade)
- "Annual price optimization" (corporate speak for raising prices 15-20% a year)
- Freemium → paid-only (Zendesk, Typeform, others)
- Bundles getting more expensive (Atlassian bundling Jira+Confluence)

4/ The uncomfortable part:
If you're a founder on a $5-10K MRR SaaS, you're now spending $500-600/mo on tools.

That's 5-10% of revenue.

Enterprise can absorb it. Bootstrapped founders can't.
