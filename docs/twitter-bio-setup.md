## Link in Bio

Since Twitter only allows one link in bio, use a link-in-bio service if you want to share multiple resources:

**Option 1: Free (Linktree)**
- Create Linktree account
- Add links: Website, Demo, Blog, Pricing
- Put `linktree.com/getpricepulse` in Twitter bio

**Option 2: Simple redirect (Carrd)**
- Create simple Carrd landing page
- Add buttons: PricePulse, Demo, Blog
- Put URL in Twitter bio

**Option 3: Just website**
- Keep Twitter bio link pointing to getpricepulse.com
- Let website nav handle rest

**Recommended:** Option 3 (simpler, keeps traffic on your site)

## Engagement Dos and Don'ts

**DO:**
- Reply to every mention/question
- Retweet customers and early supporters
- Share genuine insights from the product
- Be conversational and human
- Ask questions to spark discussion
- Share behind-the-scenes updates

**DON'T:**
- Post generic "startup tips" unrelated to pricing
- Spam your link multiple times per day
- Ignore replies or DMs
- Be overly salesy ("Buy now!")
- Copy tweets from other founders
- Post at strange hours (stick to 9-12am ET, 6-9pm ET)

---

## DM Strategy

When someone DMs you:

1. **If it's positive feedback:** Reply immediately, thank them, offer to help
2. **If it's a question:** Answer it directly, offer to jump on a call if complex
3. **If it's a complaint:** Acknowledge, take it offline, find a solution

Example good DM response:

```
Hey [Name]! Thanks so much for trying PricePulse.

What's your biggest question right now? Happy to help.

[Your Name]
```

## Hashtag Strategy

**Use sparingly** (1-2 hashtags max per tweet):

- `#SaaS` (broad, reaches lots of people)
- `#IndieHackers` (indie founder community)
- `#Bootstrap` (bootstrapped founder community)
- `#Founder` (general founder audience)
- `#Startup` (general startup audience)
- `#CompetitiveIntelligence` (specific to product)

**Avoid:** Posting tweets that are ONLY hashtags. Use hashtags naturally in sentences.
