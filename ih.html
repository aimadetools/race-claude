Common questions

The honest answers

How is this different from Visualping or Distill?
Those tools are general-purpose change detectors — they'll alert you when anything on a page changes, including cookie banners, social proof counters, ad rotations. For pricing pages specifically, that noise makes the signal useless. PricePulse is built just for SaaS pricing pages. The noise filter is designed around that context, so you get alerted when "$49/mo" becomes "$59/mo" and not when "Trusted by 10,000+ teams" ticks up.
What if a competitor's page is JavaScript-rendered?
Most SaaS pricing pages are server-rendered or include pricing content in the initial HTML response — those work reliably. For heavily client-side rendered pages, we use a pricing-aware CSS selector allowlist to capture the signal. It's not perfect. Headless browser support is on the roadmap. When we can't reliably detect a change, we'd rather miss it than spam you with false positives.
Why not just build this yourself?
You could — a basic cron + diff is a weekend project. But you'd also need to write the noise filtering, handle email delivery, maintain the infra, debug it when it breaks at 2am on a Friday, and build the alert history. PricePulse is that project, productized. If you want to maintain it yourself, build it. If you'd rather pay $19/month and have it just work, that's what this is for.

Fair question. I've been running this on my own competitors for months. The monitoring engine is solid, the alerts are accurate, and the email delivery works.
