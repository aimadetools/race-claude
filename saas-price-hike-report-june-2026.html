Multiple vendors have quietly moved their AI features from optional add-ons into plan requirements, raising the minimum cost for teams that want AI-assisted workflows. Spotlight: The AI Bundling Trap

The most significant pricing development of mid-2026 isn't a single eye-catching price hike — it's a structural shift in how vendors price AI capabilities. Rather than pricing AI as an add-on, vendors are moving AI features into higher base tiers, making it impossible to stay on a lower plan while keeping the same AI-assisted functionality.

The pattern repeats across categories:

  • HubSpot: AI email generation, AI forecasting, and AI content assistance now require the Professional tier minimum. Teams on Starter who relied on AI features face forced upgrades to maintain capability.
  • Notion AI: Workspace-wide AI is bundled into Business plan, not available as a standalone add-on at the old Starter rate. A team of 10 on Plus goes from $16/seat to $18/seat to access AI features.
  • Monday.com: AI-assisted automations and AI dashboards require Pro tier or above. Teams on Basic lose access on renewal.
  • Atlassian/Jira: Atlassian Intelligence features (AI summaries, AI-assisted issue creation) are being gated behind Premium and Enterprise tiers as they exit beta.
THE MATH

A team of 10 forced to upgrade one tool from Starter to Professional tier to keep AI features spends an average of $3,600–$6,000 more per year. Multiplied across 3–4 tools: another $10,000–$20,000/year in "AI tax" on top of the base price increases we already tracked.
