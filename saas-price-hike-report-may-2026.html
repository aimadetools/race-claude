The State of SaaS Pricing in 2026

If your SaaS bills feel heavier than two years ago, you're not imagining it. PricePulse has been tracking pricing pages across 32 major SaaS companies since early 2024. What we found: every single company we track has raised prices at least once, with the average increase sitting at +22% per company.

The driver isn't greed alone. AI feature bundling has given vendors cover to raise prices ("we added AI, so it's worth more"), while interest rate pressure has pushed post-2021 growth-at-all-costs companies to finally optimize for profit. The result: your SaaS stack is meaningfully more expensive, and most increases happened quietly — no press release, just a higher invoice.

KEY FINDING

A typical 10-person startup using GitHub Copilot, ClickUp, Slack, Asana, Figma, Semrush, Notion, and Intercom is paying approximately $4,800 more per year compared to early 2024 pricing — with no new features added to their workflow.

Spotlight: ClickUp's February 2026 Price Hike

The biggest pricing story of 2026 so far is ClickUp's February 2026 price increase. ClickUp raised their Business plan from $7/seat/month to $12/seat/month — a 71% increase per seat — but marketed it as a "$10 flat" to obscure the math. Wait, that's +43% at the plan level and +71% per seat.

The timing was strategic: ClickUp raised prices just months after gaining marketshare from Asana and Monday.com users who switched during ClickUp's "we're cheaper" marketing era. Now that users are locked in with established workflows, the price went up.

COST IMPACT EXAMPLE

15-person team on ClickUp Business: Was paying $7 × 15 × 12 = $1,260/year. Now paying $12 × 15 × 12 = $2,160/year. That's $900 more per year — with no new value delivered to existing users.

What ClickUp Users Can Do

  • Compare with alternatives: Asana vs ClickUp vs Monday — Monday.com's Standard plan is $12/user, Asana Premium is $13.49/user. ClickUp's cost advantage is now minimal.
  • Downgrade plan: ClickUp's Unlimited plan ($7/seat) may be sufficient for teams not using Business-tier features like custom roles and time tracking reports.
  • Set a monitoring alert: Use PricePulse to be notified the next time any of your tools change pricing — before the invoice arrives.

What Should You Do About SaaS Inflation?

The bad news: you can't negotiate public list prices. The good news: there are three concrete actions that reduce your exposure.

1. Audit before renewal, not after

Most contracts auto-renew annually. Companies count on you not checking pricing between renewals. Set a reminder 60 days before each subscription renews to check whether pricing has changed and whether alternatives are now cheaper.

2. Compare the real alternatives

Several vendors have kept pricing stable or even gotten cheaper relative to bloated alternatives:

  • Linear vs Jira — $10/seat vs $9.30/seat, better UX for engineering teams. Compare →
  • Make.com vs Zapier — 81% cheaper per operation at scale. Compare →
